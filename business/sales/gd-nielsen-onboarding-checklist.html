Create Your Squarespace Account
3 minutes
Your new site will live on Squarespace 7.1, which is what I'll use to rebuild and launch gdnielsen.com. You'll create the account under your email so GD Nielsen owns it outright, then add me as a contributor so I can build and maintain it.
  • Go to squarespace.com and click Get Started
  • Sign up with dk@gdnielsen.com
    You can skip the template picker, I'll handle the design.
  • Once in, go to:
    Settings → Permissions & Ownership → Invite Contributor
  • Enter info@mattanthonyphoto.com, set role to Administrator, and send the invite
    You stay the site owner. I just get backend access to build and maintain the site.

Connect Your Social Platforms
8 minutes
Two things happen here. First, you'll create your Zernio account, the tool I use to schedule and publish your content. Then you'll add me as an admin on Meta Business Manager so I can manage your Instagram directly for analytics and engagement.
Your Zernio signup link

  • Once logged in, click "Add Account" and connect Instagram

  • If you have a Pinterest business account, add it too
    Pinterest acts as a passive SEO engine for custom home builders. Every pin links back to your site. If you don't have one yet, I'll set one up.

Now add me to Meta Business Manager. Zernio handles scheduling, but I also need direct admin access to run analytics, engagement, and troubleshooting.
  • Go to business.facebook.com and log in

    Settings → People → Add People

The two pages you'll use most
✎

Approvals

Every social post I draft lands here before it goes live. You'll see the images, caption, and hashtags for each one. Three options. Approve if it's good to go, Request Revision with specific notes and I'll rebuild it, or Decline to start fresh. Nothing publishes without your sign off.

📅

Content Calendar

A monthly view of every post, past and upcoming. Color coded by status so you can see at a glance what's published, what's scheduled, and what's waiting on your review. Click any post to see the full details.

Your weekly rhythm

  1. I draft your posts and they appear in Approvals
  2. You review each one. Takes about 5 to 10 minutes with a coffee
  3. Approve the ones that are good to go
  4. If something feels off, hit Request Revision and tell me what to change. I'll rebuild it with your notes
  5. Approved posts publish automatically on schedule
