01

Wilson Residence, Oles Cove.

5383 Oles Cove Road  ·  Second Nature Designs  ·  Completed  ·  0.928 ha waterfront parcel

A finished house with subjects on site. I've shot here before, which removes half the unknowns. What I don't know is the design story — the decisions the drawings can't tell me. That's what the pre-shoot conversation is for.

Known
  • Completed waterfront build on private acreage. Rock bluff to the north, water to the west.
  • Subjects (the Wilsons) on site — environmental portraits, not pure architecture.
  • Previous completion reel shot here. Known site, known light behaviour.
  • Window vendors Innotech and Kolbe have indicated interest in cost-share participation.
Pre-shoot
conversation

Twenty minutes, any time before the 14th. The questions I'd ask:

  1. Brief. What did the Wilsons ask for. What was non-negotiable.
  2. Siting. What dictated the position on the parcel. Prevailing wind, view corridor, sun, existing trees.
  3. One thing. If you walked a stranger through, where would you stop. What would you show.
  4. Material. Cladding, roof, interior joinery, stone. Where did the budget go, where did you hold back.
  5. Occupation. How do they actually use the house. Where do they drink coffee. Where do they eat in summer.
  6. Landscape. Mature vs. freshly planted. Anything in bloom in mid-May.
  7. Publication. Shelter magazine candidate or internal portfolio piece. Different edit either way.
Intended
frames
  • Siting, from water. Drone at low altitude, low tide if it reads. The house in its parcel.
  • Approach. Driveway to threshold. Two or three frames that build the arrival.
  • Primary axis. Living volume out to the water. The view the plan was organised around.
  • Portraits. Three to five. Island, deck, dock, a quiet interior. Neutral wardrobe.
  • Material close-ups. Three to four, where the money is.
  • Reel, 15–30s. Approach push, a human beat, a window reveal, an evening exterior. Vertical, licensed score.
Logistics
  • Wilsons on site two to three hours. Wardrobe note: solids, no logos, no primaries.
  • Tide chart for the waterside frames. Confirmed before shoot day.
  • Drone airspace over the cove verified.

02

Madeira Park Residence, Johnstone Road.

4976 Johnstone Road  ·  Keck Architecture + Design  ·  Completed, unfurnished  ·  Waterfront harbour

A designed house, not a spec house. The drawings read like a Keck project — restrained, declarative, Scandinavian in reference. This one wants to be photographed on its own terms, not the listing's.

Reading
the parti

Two gabled volumes, east and west, are knit together by a lower linking volume with a double-height stair. A continuous skylight band lets light down into the plan from above. The east wing cantilevers toward the water with a glass-guarded deck. A standing-seam metal roof and vertical wood siding form the only exterior vocabulary. Nothing decorative. No applied trim.

The house reads almost agrarian from the approach. From the water, the cantilever changes the conversation — suddenly the building is a modern piece of architecture, not a gabled cabin.

On staging

No landscaping, no furniture, listing-prep reality. An honest interior will read cold. There is no soft layer to warm the architecture.

My position: shoot the house honest first. One sailboat at the dock is the only staging that belongs on a shoot day — it gives the waterside exterior a scale anchor and a tenure.

Furnished versions are a post-production exercise, not a shoot decision. Select three to five interior frames after the fact and we'll stage them digitally for the listing. The portfolio edit and the MLS edit become two separate things, drawn from the same negatives.

Intended
frames, exterior
  • Gable elevation, road side. Head-on, symmetrical, neutral light.
  • Gable elevation, water side. From shoreline or water, drone at eave-line.
  • Approach. Driveway arrival, flush-siding-to-concrete detail.
  • Deck cantilever. From below, waterside, emphasising the float over the workshop.
  • Stepped concrete retaining. The site moves down the rock in stages.
  • Parti shot. Drone, 30° angle from water. The two wings and the link, legible in one frame.
Intended
frames, interior
  • Stair volume, up. Lower floor looking up through the curtain wall.
  • Stair volume, down. Upper landing, the descent and the skylight above.
  • Kitchen / living / deck. Framed through the deck opening toward water.
  • Skylight cast. Mid-morning, watch for harsh bars across surfaces.
  • Thresholds. Wing-to-stair transitions, east and west. The reveal detailing.
  • Soffit. Where the clear-finish wood carries from exterior eave to interior ceiling.
Reel, 15–30s
  • Approach, panning to reveal the gable geometry.
  • Stair volume, morning light through the curtain wall. Single beat.
  • Deck cantilever toward water, dolly.
  • Drone pullback at close. The harbour context.
  • Vertical cut, licensed score. No cinematic swell.
Logistics
  • Tide chart for waterside frames and the dock stage.
  • Confirm sailboat availability and berth timing.
  • Drone airspace cleared around Madeira Park harbour.
  • Site cleaned: construction dust, ladder marks on walkouts, tape residue on glass.
  • MEP tags removed from vents, glazing, appliances.
  • Blinds or window treatments confirmed if any install pre-shoot.
