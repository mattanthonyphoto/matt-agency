Discovery call · 20 minutes

The design story is the thing I can’t pull from a floor plan. Questions I’ll bring:

  1. Brief & intent. What did the Wilsons ask for? What was the non-negotiable?
  2. Site moves. What dictated the siting — prevailing wind, view corridor, sun path, existing trees?
  3. Signature moment. If Wilson had to walk one person through the house and show them one thing, where would they stop?
  4. Material story. Exterior cladding, roof, interior millwork, stone. Where did you spend, where did you restrain?
  5. Lifestyle beats. How do the Wilsons actually use this house? Coffee spot, entertaining zone, dog’s corner, kids’ hangout.
  6. Landscape. Who did it, what’s mature vs. freshly planted, anything flowering in mid-May.
  7. Publication potential. Shelter-mag candidate, or marketing/portfolio piece? That changes the edit.

Project 02
Madeira Park — Johnstone Rd.
4976 Johnstone Rd · Keck Architecture + Design · Waterfront harbour

This is clearly the more architecturally restrained of the two — a designed home, not a spec home. The drawings tell me it wants to be photographed like a Keck project, not like a listing.

Parti · what the drawings tell me

Massing

  • Two gabled wings — east & west — linked by a lower central volume
  • East wing projects toward the water; west wing anchors the driveway
  • Pure vertical siding, no fussy trim — agrarian / Scandinavian read
  • Standing-seam metal roof, vented ridge and eaves detailed cleanly

The signature move

  • Double-height staircase volume between the two wings
  • Glass curtain wall on one face — the “staircase / atrium” you flagged
  • Continuous skylight run above the upper floor
  • This is almost certainly the hero interior frame

Upper floor

  • Kitchen / living / dining, open plan
  • Deck cantilever over the lower workshop — glass guardrail, stainless cap
  • East wing living opens to the deck and the water
  • Flush window sills, no reveal clutter

Lower floor & site

  • Bedrooms, baths, laundry, workshop/storage
  • Walkout at the waterside via the stepped grade
  • Wood soffits, clear finish — warm reflected light under the eave
  • CIP concrete planters step down the steep waterfront rock

The staging question

No landscaping, no furnishings, listing-prep reality. Interiors will read cold and empty without intervention; there’s no soft layer (plants, textiles, art) to warm the architecture. My take:

1. Sailboat at the dock — yes. It’s a scale anchor for the waterfront exteriors and reads as “a place that belongs on the water.” Coordinate tide.

2. AI furnishings — treat as a post-production add-on, not a shoot-day call. Shoot the space clean and honest, then select 3–5 hero interior frames for AI staging (bed, living seating, dining, kitchen dressing) as a separate deliverable. That way the architectural portfolio version and the listing version are two distinct edits off the same shoot. Happy to quote separately once we see the empty frames.

3. Real props we can bring. One piece of driftwood, a stoneware vase, a linen throw. Small things that give scale without lying about the finish-out.

Shot priorities · exterior

  • Hero gable-end elevation (road side) — symmetrical, dead-on, neutral light
  • Hero gable-end elevation (water side) — from the water or shoreline, drone at eave-line
  • Approach frame from driveway, showing the flush-wall siding-to-concrete detail
  • Cantilever deck from below on the waterside, emphasizing the float
  • Stepped planter / retaining wall sequence down the slope
  • Drone top-down + 30° angle showing the two-wing parti — the shot that explains the design

Shot priorities · interior

  • Stair volume from the lower floor looking up through the curtain wall — the money shot
  • Stair volume from the upper landing looking down
  • Kitchen / living open-plan, framed through the deck opening toward water
  • Skylight light-cast frames (mid-morning, watch for harsh bars)
  • Threshold shots at the wing-to-stair transitions — east and west
  • Ceiling / soffit details where the wood soffit carries indoor to outdoor

Reel beats · 15–30s

  • Approach from driveway → pan to reveal gable geometry
  • Interior stair volume light reveal — probably the best single beat
  • Cantilever deck → water view dolly
  • Drone pullback at close — house sits in harbour context
  • Licensed music, vertical cut — lean Scandi-minimal, not cinematic swell

Logistics

  • Tide chart for waterfront frames and dock staging
  • Confirm sailboat availability and berth timing for the shoot day
  • Drone — confirm airspace around Madeira Park harbour
  • Clean-up pass needed — construction dust, ladder marks, tape residue on glass
  • Clear all MEP tags (HRV stickers, window tags) before we arrive
  • Blinds / window coverings, if any go in pre-shoot — confirm
