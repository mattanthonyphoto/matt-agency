# The Spouse / Office Manager Channel

*Tactical plan for reaching the actual marketing decision-maker in builder businesses*

---

## Why This Matters

In the $1M-$5M range, the builder is on-site all day. The **spouse or office manager** handles:
- Website updates
- Instagram / social media
- Award submissions
- Marketing materials and proposals
- Client communications
- Often bookkeeping

They're your actual buyer. They understand visual content. They're frustrated by bad photos. They're already looking for a solution.

---

## How to Identify Them

### Method 1: Company Website "Team" Page
Look for titles: Office Manager, Director of Operations, Marketing Coordinator, Business Manager, Co-Owner/Partner, or the owner's spouse with a different title.

### Method 2: LinkedIn
Search company name, filter by current employees. In a 5-15 person company, the marketing/office person stands out with a non-construction title.

### Method 3: Instagram
Check who runs the company account. Bio sometimes says "Run by [Name]" or the posting style reveals a non-builder voice (polished captions, consistent schedule, hashtag strategy).

### Method 4: Facebook
Builder's personal profile often shows the spouse. Spouse's profile often lists the company in their work history, or they're tagged in company posts.

### Method 5: Google
Search "[Company] office manager" or "[Company] marketing." Local business articles, charity events, and community features often surface the spouse.

### Method 6: Call the Office
"Who handles your marketing and website? I'd love to connect with them." Direct and normal.

## How the Pitch Differs

| To the builder | To the spouse/office manager |
|---|---|
| "Your craft deserves to be documented" | "I can make your website and Instagram job so much easier" |
| "Photography is 0.23% of project cost" | "One shoot gives you 6+ months of content across every platform" |
| "Your competitors are investing" | "I saw [competitor]'s latest post — your projects are just as good, they just need to be shown that way" |
| Frame as documentation | Frame as making their workflow easier |
| Speak craft language | Speak marketing + content language |

**They don't need to be convinced photography matters.** They already know. They need confidence you can deliver, and ammunition to justify the spend to the builder.

## Helping Them Sell It Internally

The spouse/office manager often needs to justify the expense to the builder. Give them tools:

### One-Page "Why Professional Photography" PDF
Include:
- Before/after of a builder website with and without professional images
- ROI angle: "One shoot generates 30-50 images usable across website, IG, Google Business, awards, print materials, and proposals for 12+ months"
- Awards angle: "Georgie entries require professional photography. iPhone photos don't advance."
- Competitive angle: "Here's what [competitor they respect] is doing"
- Clear starting price so the builder can make a quick yes/no

### The Forwardable Quote
After your initial call, send a clean, simple proposal they can forward to the builder with a note: "I talked to this photographer, I think we should do this. Here's what it costs."

### The Business Decision Frame
"This isn't about pretty pictures. Your projects are your portfolio. Every $1M+ home is a marketing asset that can generate the next client. But only if it's documented properly."

## Where They Gather

### Associations
- **NAHB Professional Women in Building (PWB) Council** — 14,000 members, education + networking
- **CHBA BC** local chapter events — spouses often attend alongside builders
- **Sea to Sky CHBA** — local networking where the "office side" of builder businesses participates

### Events
- **Georgie Awards Gala** — many builders bring their spouse. If you meet both, **prioritize connecting with the spouse.** They're the one who will remember you and act on follow-up.
- CHBA BC local HBA networking events and mixers
- Vancouver Home + Design Show
- Interior Design Show West
- Local chamber of commerce events in builder-heavy communities

### Online
- Facebook groups: local community groups, CHBA BC page, builder association groups
- #WICWeek (Women in Construction Week) community on Instagram

## Tactical Implementation

### Immediate (This Week)
1. For every builder on your cold email list and Georgie target list — check: who runs their Instagram? Is there a spouse/partner/office manager on their website?
2. Add a "Marketing Contact" column to your tracking: name, title, channel (IG handle, email)
3. Follow company Instagrams and start engaging

### April-May
4. For Georgie Gala targets — identify the spouse/OM for each builder
5. At the gala, if you meet builder AND spouse, connect with both
6. In follow-up messages, send a separate follow-up directly to the spouse/OM

### Ongoing
7. When cold emails to builders don't get responses, try the office manager as secondary approach. Different person, different channel, different angle.
8. Create the one-page PDF as a leave-behind asset for this audience.
