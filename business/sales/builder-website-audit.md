# Sea-to-Sky & Vancouver Builder Website Audit

*Conducted March 26, 2026 — point-in-time snapshot*

---

## Tier 1 — Best Prospects (Great Builds, Bad/Missing Photography)

### CMC Homes (Coastal Mountain Custom Homes)
- **Location:** Whistler / Squamish / Kadenwood
- **Website:** cmchomes.ca
- **Build quality:** Luxury custom, 16+ projects, 20+ employees
- **Photography:** Website loads placeholder SVGs instead of actual photos. No awards displayed, no video, inconsistent quality across what is shown.
- **Instagram:** Unknown/minimal presence
- **Assessment:** **Single biggest opportunity in the corridor.** Luxury Kadenwood builds being represented by placeholder images. Massive gap between build quality and visual presentation.

### SMC Contracting
- **Location:** Sea-to-Sky corridor
- **Website:** Bare-minimum Wix site
- **Build quality:** 30+ years experience, craftsman approach (one project at a time)
- **Photography:** No visible professional photography
- **Instagram:** 437 followers
- **Assessment:** Strong craft credentials but zero visual presence. Budget may be limited given small operation. Good candidate for a single project shoot to demonstrate value.

### Vista Coastal Builders
- **Location:** Squamish
- **Website:** Luxury-feeling template (gold accents, Playfair Display font, portfolio infrastructure)
- **Build quality:** Rebranded 2021, has a wood fabrication shop
- **Photography:** Portfolio content appears thin despite having the container ready
- **Instagram:** Unknown
- **Awards:** None visible
- **Assessment:** The website container is ready — it just needs professional content to fill it. Wood fab shop is a great visual story waiting to be told.

---

## Tier 2 — Medium Opportunities

### Schreyer Construction
- **Location:** Whistler / Sea-to-Sky
- **Specialty:** Passive House
- **Photography:** Only 5 projects shown, no video
- **Assessment:** Their specialty (Passive House) is visually under-differentiated. Professional photography could help communicate what makes high-performance building special.

### Blackfish Homes
- **Location:** North Vancouver
- **Website:** Template website, no blog, no content strategy
- **Photography:** 24 projects shown. Photography decent but could be elevated.
- **Instagram:** 6K followers
- **Assessment:** Volume of work suggests budget exists. Photography upgrade + content strategy would move the needle.

### Alair Homes (Squamish/Vancouver/Whistler)
- **Franchise model:** Local partners need local content that corporate template doesn't provide
- **Note:** **2026 Georgie finalist** with "Arrowhead" project (Squamish) — two categories
- **Assessment:** Franchise creates both constraint (corporate brand guidelines) and opportunity (local partner needs differentiation). Being a Georgie finalist in Squamish makes them a natural conversation target.

### RDC Fine Homes

### Hasler Homes

### Blueline Contracting
- Strong photography, awards presence
- 2026 Georgie finalist ("Tree House", $6M-$10M category)

### My House Design Build (Vancouver)
- 100+ awards, gold standard for builder marketing in BC

### Lanefab Design/Build (Vancouver)
- NYT, Dwell, Forbes coverage. Already at publication level.

### Kindred Custom Homes (Vancouver)
- Credits **Upper-Left Photography** on site
- 2026 Georgie finalist ("Sword Fern Sanctuary", $6M-$10M)

### Okanagan Builders (Knowlton, Lake Valley, Bercum)
- All three have established photographer relationships
- Okanagan is ahead of Sea-to-Sky in this regard

---

## Key Findings

1. **Only 3 of 15 builders credit their photographer anywhere on their site.**
2. **Zero Sea-to-Sky builders credit a photographer.** The local market has no locked-in relationships.
3. **The Okanagan is ahead of Sea-to-Sky** in established photographer relationships.
4. **CMC Homes is the biggest single opportunity** — luxury builds, placeholder images.
5. **Alair Homes Squamish** is the best warm lead — Georgie finalist, local, franchise partner likely needs differentiation.
