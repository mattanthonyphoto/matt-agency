# Interior Designer Retainer Model

*Instagram-first retainer for residential designers completing 6-12 projects/year*

---

## How It Differs

| | Designer | Builder | Architect |
|---|---|---|---|
| Primary purpose | Instagram + portfolio + awards | Portfolio + sales + awards | Portfolio + awards + publications |
| Image style | Intimate, detail-heavy, editorial | Wide angles, lifestyle | Spatial, formal, publication-grade |
| Urgency | HIGH — Instagram reveal window | Medium | Low |
| Detail shots | Essential (50%+ of deliverables) | Nice-to-have (10-20%) | Minimal (5-10%) |
| Vertical format | Must-have | Useful but secondary | Rarely needed |
| Turnaround | 24-48hr sneak peek critical | 2-3 weeks OK | 3-6 weeks OK |
| Styling involvement | Must understand styling | Basic staging | No styling needed |

## The Trust Ladder

### Stage 1: Cost-Share Entry
You're shooting for a builder. Offer the designer a license. They get professional images at $1,200-$1,500 instead of $3,000.

### Stage 2: Detail Session Gateway
Offer a 2-hour detail/vignette session ($500-$800, 6-8 images). Low-risk way to test the relationship.

### Stage 3: First Full Project
They liked the cost-share or detail session. Now they hire you directly for their next completion.

### Stage 4: Repeat + Awards
Second or third project. You help them get published or win a SHINE Award.

### Stage 5: The Retainer Conversation
"Your Instagram needs consistent content. Let me formalize what we're already doing — guaranteed coverage, social-ready deliverables, priority scheduling, and I handle cost-share coordination on every project."

## Key Differentiators vs. Other Retainers

### Speed
Same-day or next-day sneak peeks. The Instagram reveal window is everything for designers.

### Format-First Delivery
Images delivered in ALL social formats — not just high-res horizontals they have to crop. Vertical (4:5), square (1:1), Stories (9:16), Pinterest (2:3).

### Content Planning
Not just "here are your photos" but "here's how to sequence these across 3 weeks of posts."

### Detail Shot Volume
50%+ of deliverables should be details and vignettes. This is what designers need and what architectural photographers often underdeliver.

### Awards Pipeline
Every shoot is a potential award entry. Submission-ready packages for IDIBC SHINE, Western Living DOTY, Georgie interior design categories.
