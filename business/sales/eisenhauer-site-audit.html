01 — Technical SEO

Yoast is installed.
Nobody configured it.

Eisenhauer.build runs WordPress with Yoast SEO, the industry-standard plugin. Every field Yoast provides — meta descriptions, schema markup, Open Graph, focus keywords — is empty or broken. The tools are there. They've never been used.

Critical Failures
✗
Zero Meta Descriptions (0 of 35+ pages)
Not a single page on the entire site has a meta description. Google auto-generates snippets from page content, which means every search result is an uncontrolled first impression. Yoast provides the fields. They were never filled in.
✗
Zero Structured Data / Schema Markup
No LocalBusiness, Organization, Article, FAQ, BreadcrumbList, or any schema type. Google doesn't know Eisenhauer Woodworks is a builder, where it's located, or what it does. The FAQ page has 6 structured Q&As that could generate rich snippets in search results — but there's no FAQ schema to enable them.
✗
No Google Analytics or Tag Manager
No analytics tracking detected on any page. The marketing team cannot measure ROI because they have no data. No traffic data, no user behavior, no conversion tracking. The Inquire thank-you page has no conversion pixel — form submissions aren't being counted.
✗
53 Images Missing Alt Text on Two Flagship Projects
The Coulter Residence (28 images) and Morrison Farmhouse (25 images) — the two most important projects on the site — have zero alt text on every single image. Estimated 60-80+ images sitewide are invisible to search engines.
✗
Only 5 of 35+ Pages Indexed by Google
A site:eisenhauer.build search returns only 5 results. The services page, about page, FAQ, build guide, and 4+ portfolio pages are not indexed by Google. They exist on the server but are invisible to search.
Garbled Title Tags
✗
Inquire: "Inquire Homework's & Woodworks"
Garbled, nonsensical text. Likely AI-generated or auto-suggested and never proofread. This is what prospects see in Google search results.
✗
Taqueria: "Taqueria el Corazon Bulid Now"
A typo visible in Google SERPs. "Bulid" instead of "Build." This has been live in search results for over a year.
✗
Larch Drive: "Dr Larch | Larch Drive"
Nonsensical. "Dr Larch" appears to be auto-generated text that was never reviewed.
✗
Build Guide Uses About Page's Title Tag
Duplicate title: both the About page and Build Guide show "Eisenhauer Woodworks About | Eisenhauer Luxury construction." Google penalizes duplicate titles.
✗
Demo Shop Test Page Is Live and Indexed
"Eisenhauer Woodworks Demo Shop | Demo Shop Woodworks" — a leftover development page that says "This is a custom category page for Shop." Live, indexable, unprofessional.
Additional Technical Issues
✗
Three Legacy Domains Still Live
eisenhauer.build (current), jeisenhauerwoodworks.squarespace.com (old site, lists Vancouver), and jeisenhauerwoodworks.com — all indexed by Google. No redirects. Authority fragmented across three domains. The old site shows a Vancouver address while the current one says Kimberley.
!
robots.txt Has crawl-delay: 60
Tells Bing crawlers to wait 60 seconds between requests, dramatically slowing index coverage on Microsoft's search engine. No sitemap URL declared in robots.txt either.
!
No Open Graph Tags
No og:title, og:description, or og:image on any page. When someone shares a project on Facebook or LinkedIn, the preview is unpredictable.
!
No WebP Images, No Lazy Loading
All images served as JPEG/PNG. No modern format optimization. No lazy loading detected on gallery pages with 25+ images.

Technical SEO Bottom Line

The site has the tools (WordPress + Yoast) but none of the configuration. Zero meta descriptions, zero schema, zero analytics, garbled title tags with visible typos, and three legacy domains splitting authority. These are fundamental items that should have been addressed when the site launched. The marketing team cannot measure ROI because they never installed tracking.

03 — Local SEO

No Google Business Profile.
No map presence.

When a homeowner in Kimberley searches "custom home builder near me," the first thing they see is the Google Map Pack. Eisenhauer Woodworks doesn't appear in it.

Google Business Profile
✗
No Google Business Profile Confirmed
Multiple search queries returned no Google Maps listing, no star rating, no review count. Either the GBP doesn't exist, is unclaimed, or has zero activity. This is free, takes 30 minutes, and is the single most impactful local SEO fix.
NAP Inconsistency
✗
Three Different Cities Across Listings
Website says Kimberley. ZoomInfo and Visit Kaslo say Kaslo. Old Squarespace site says Vancouver. Three conflicting locations across the web destroy Google's trust in location data.
!
Phone Number Signals Wrong Location
250-353 is a Kaslo/Nelson area exchange. Kimberley uses 250-427. The phone number itself signals the wrong geography to search engines.
!
Four Business Name Variations
"Eisenhauer Woodworks," "Eisenhauer Woodworks Inc," "J. Eisenhauer Woodworks," and "J Eisenhauer Woodworks" appear across different directories. Inconsistent naming dilutes citation value.
Zero Location Pages
✗
Serves 6 Cities. Ranks in 1.
Eisenhauer claims to serve Kimberley, Cranbrook, Fernie, Invermere, Radium Hot Springs, and Kaslo. There are zero location-specific landing pages for any of them. Munter has dedicated pages for three cities and ranks in all three.

Local SEO Bottom Line

No Google Business Profile, conflicting addresses across the web, and zero location pages. The foundation of local search visibility doesn't exist. A builder charging $750K–$2.5M per project should be the first result when someone in their service area searches "custom home builder near me."

05 — Photography & Visual Assets

The brand says "built by hand."
The images don't show it.

Eisenhauer's entire identity rests on handcraft, Wabi-Sabi aesthetics, and a four-generation woodworking heritage. But there's a fundamental disconnect: the story is told entirely in text. The photography doesn't capture the craft, the process, or the human side of building.

What Exists
✓
Two Projects with Professional Photography
The Coulter Residence (28 photos) and Morrison Farmhouse (25 photos) have professional-quality compositions. These carry the visual weight of the entire site.
!
Morrison Photos Sourced from Flickr
Filenames contain Flickr IDs (e.g., 53075783185_9c807e0c06_k.jpeg). The "_k" suffix is Flickr's "Large 2048" size — not full-resolution originals. This is not a professional delivery pipeline.
!
Remaining Projects Are Thin
Larch Drive (10 images), Chan Residence (11), Amundsen Road (8–11), McKinney-Behn (9). Only 6 custom home projects shown after nearly 20 years. Either work isn't being photographed, or it isn't being shown.
What's Missing
✗
Zero Twilight / Dusk Photography
The gold standard for luxury home marketing. For $800K–$1.8M mountain homes with Kootenay views, twilight exteriors are non-negotiable. Not one image on the site.
✗
Zero Aerial / Drone Photography
Mountain context, valley settings, property scale, site integration — none of this is captured. Aerial photography shows how a home belongs to its landscape.
✗
Zero Process / Craftsmanship Photography
This is the single biggest brand contradiction. The homepage says "Hand Built." The About page describes four generations of carpenters. But there is not one image showing hands at work, timber being selected, joints being cut, or any of the craft that defines this brand.
✗
Zero Lifestyle Photography
Every home feels empty and unlived-in. No warmth, no people, no sense of "what it feels like to live here." Lifestyle photography humanizes the work and creates emotional connection.
✗
No Team or Headshot Photography
Only one construction-site candid of Jeremy. No professional headshot. No team photos despite having a year-round crew of 6–8. The people behind the brand are invisible.

The Brand Story Gap

Eisenhauer's story — four generations of carpenters, Inside Passage fine woodworking training, Wabi-Sabi philosophy, "built by hand" — is genuinely differentiated. No other builder in the East Kootenays has this narrative. But the story exists only in text. Professional architectural and documentary photography would bridge the gap between what the brand says and what it shows.

07 — Content & Website

The blog died 10 months ago.
The content is thin.

Eisenhauer's "Build Guide" blog published 12 solid articles between July 2024 and June 2025. Nothing since. The services page is 180 words. Portfolio pages average 200 words with no specs, no client stories, no CTAs.

Content Thinness
✗
Services Page: 180 Words
A luxury builder's services page should be 800–1,500+ words covering process, materials, certifications, and service areas. Munter's Kimberley location page alone is 1,200–1,400 words with testimonials and a process breakdown.
✗
Blog Dead Since June 2025
12 posts total, average 575 words (too thin for competitive keywords). The content was genuinely useful when it existed — cost transparency, builder selection advice. But 10+ months of silence kills SEO momentum.
✗
Inquire Page Says "Now Booking 2025–2026 Builds"
Six months out of date. Signals the site isn't actively maintained.
!
No Project Specs or Case Studies
Portfolio pages have photos and ~200 words of narrative. No square footage, bedroom count, build timeline, budget range, or material specifications. No client testimonials on project pages. No CTAs.
!
Total Estimated Site Content: ~8,000–10,000 Words
A competitive luxury builder site should have 30,000–50,000+ words across services, locations, projects, and educational content.

Content Bottom Line

The site has a strong brand voice when it's actually writing. The Wabi-Sabi philosophy, the family legacy, the material sensitivity — it's compelling. But there isn't enough of it. Thin pages, a dead blog, and no email capture mean the website generates almost no organic traffic and converts none of it into a relationship.

10 — What I'd Fix First

The action plan.
Priority order.

Photography is the prerequisite for nearly every item on this list. Awards require professional imagery. Press pitches need hero shots. Social content needs a visual pipeline. Reviews need projects worth showing. It all starts with the same thing.

1

Professional Photography of Top 3 Projects

Twilight exteriors, aerial/drone, interior details, material close-ups, and process/craftsmanship documentation. One shoot creates the raw material for everything below: awards, press, social, website, directories.

2

Enter the CHBA Keystone & Georgie Awards

Eisenhauer is already a CHBA member. The Coulter Residence and Morrison Farmhouse are award-worthy. Professional photography is the submission requirement. One award win changes competitive positioning overnight.

3

Build the Review & Content Infrastructure

Google Business Profile (30 minutes, free). Past client reviews on Houzz. Testimonials on the website. Project case studies with specs. Content that turns craft into credibility.

How Photography Feeds Every Channel

Awards: CHBA Keystone and Georgie submissions are judged primarily on imagery. No professional photos = no entry.

Press: Kootenay Mountain Culture, Western Living, and Kootenay Business all require hero imagery to run a feature.

Social: One professional shoot generates 6–12 months of Instagram, LinkedIn, Pinterest, and YouTube content.

Website: Project pages with professional photography, material details, and twilight exteriors convert browsers into inquiries.

Directories: Houzz, Google Business, and every other platform perform better with professional project imagery.

One relationship. One investment. Every channel benefits.
