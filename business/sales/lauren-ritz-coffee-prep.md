## Conversation Flow

### 1. Open — Thank Her for the Review (2 min)
"Lauren, first — thank you for the Google review. That genuinely means a lot. Those reviews are becoming one of the most important things for my business."

*Don't linger. Move to genuine curiosity.*

### 2. Ask About Current Projects (10 min)
Listen for:
- What she's working on right now
- Any projects completing in the next 3-6 months
- Whether she's working with any builders you know (or should know)
- Pain points — is she struggling to get good photos of her work?

Key questions:
- "What are you most excited about right now?"
- "Are any of those wrapping up this spring or summer?"
- "Who are you working with on the build side?"

### 3. The Awards & Publications Angle — NEW (5 min)
This is the new differentiator. Plant this seed naturally:

"One thing I've been building out is helping my clients get their work into awards and publications. Georgie Awards, Western Living, Dwell — that kind of thing. Most designers and builders I work with have incredible projects but never submit them anywhere. I'm starting to handle that whole process — the photography is already shot to spec, so I just prepare the entries and manage submissions."

If she's interested, mention:
- **Western Living Designers of the Year** — deadline is April 17, 2026 (3 weeks away). Entry fee is $150 early bird. She would submit as the designer, and your photos make the submission strong.
- **Georgie Awards** have an Interior Design category
- **One project photographed right = 3-5 credentials** (award entry, magazine pitch, ArchDaily, social content)

*Don't hard-sell. Frame it as: "I'm doing this for my builder clients already, and it works just as well for designers."*

### 4. Creative Partner Mention — Light Touch (3 min)
Only if the conversation is going well and she's talking about multiple projects:

"I've been moving toward a model where I work as an ongoing creative partner with a few firms — handling photography, content, and now awards submissions on a retainer basis instead of project-by-project. It ends up being way more efficient for both sides."

If she asks for details:
- Monthly retainer, predictable cost
- Photography for every project as it completes
- Social media content from each shoot
- Awards and publication submissions included
- "I just put together a proposal for one of my builder clients — happy to share the structure if you want to see what it looks like."

*Don't share the Balmoral proposal directly. Offer to build one tailored to her.*

### 5. Ask for Referrals (2 min)
"Who else in the design community should I be talking to? Other designers, architects — anyone who's doing great work but not getting it documented or submitted?"

Target: 1-2 names. Designers tend to know other designers.

### 6. Close — Next Steps (2 min)
"This was great. I'll send you a quick follow-up email today. If any of those projects are getting close to completion, let's get a shoot on the calendar."
