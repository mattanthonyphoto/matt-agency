## Project 01 — 5383 Oles Cove Rd (Wilson Residence)

### What I know so far
- Waterfront acreage, completed build
- You want subjects (the Wilsons) in frame — lifestyle, not just architecture
- We shot a completion reel here previously
- Window vendors (Innotech, Kolbe) have flagged interest in co-marketing

### What I need from you (Oles Cove discovery call — 20 min)
The design story is the thing I can't pull from a floor plan. Questions I'll bring:

1. **Brief & intent** — What did the Wilsons ask for? What was the non-negotiable?
2. **Site moves** — What dictated the siting? Prevailing wind, view corridor, sun path, existing trees?
3. **Signature moment** — If Wilson had to walk one person through the house and show them *one* thing, where would they stop?
4. **Material story** — Exterior cladding, roof, interior millwork, stone. Where did you spend, where did you restrain?
5. **Lifestyle beats** — How do the Wilsons actually *use* this house? Coffee spot, entertaining zone, dog's corner, kids' hangout.
6. **Landscape** — Who did it, what's mature vs. freshly planted, anything flowering in mid-May.
7. **Publication potential** — Is this a shelter-mag candidate, or is it a marketing/portfolio piece? That changes the edit.

### Shot priorities (to confirm on call)
- **Hero exterior** — wide establishing shot that places the house on the waterfront. Likely golden hour, likely drone-assisted for the siting context.
- **Approach sequence** — driveway → entry, two to three frames that build anticipation.
- **Primary living → water** — the view corridor the design was built around.
- **Subjects in frame** — 3–5 environmental portraits: Wilsons at the kitchen island, on the deck with water behind, on the dock/path, quiet candid. Matched wardrobe palette.
- **Detail layer** — 3–4 tight frames of the material choices that cost real money (joinery, stone, hardware, millwork transitions).
- **Reel beats (15–30s)** — approach push-in, a human moment, a window-reveal tracking shot, an exterior golden-hour sweep. Licensed music, vertical cut.

### Logistics

- Wardrobe note to Wilsons: solids, neutral palette, avoid logos/bright primaries
- Tide chart for waterfront framing — I'll check the May 14 swing

## Project 02 — 4976 Johnstone Rd (Madeira Park Residence)

Architect: **Keck Architecture+Design** (Scott Keck, Gibsons). This is clearly the more architecturally restrained of the two — a designed home, not a spec home. The drawings tell me this one wants to be photographed like a Keck project, not like a listing.

### What I pulled from the drawings

**Parti / massing**
- Two gabled wings (east + west) linked by a lower central volume
- East wing projects toward the water; west wing anchors the driveway side
- Gables are pure, vertical-sided, no fussy trim — reads almost agrarian / Scandinavian
- Roof: standing-seam metal, prefinished galvanized, with vented ridge and vented eaves detailed cleanly

**The signature move — central stair volume**
- Double-height staircase between the two wings
- Glass curtain wall on one face (north or water side — confirm on site)
- This is the "staircase / atrium" you mentioned in your email
- Skylights above the upper floor run the length of the roof
- This volume is almost certainly the hero interior frame

**Upper floor**
- Kitchen / living / dining open plan
- Deck cantilever over the lower workshop/storage — glass guardrail with stainless cap, reads floating
- East wing living opens to the deck and the water
- Flush window sills, no reveal clutter

**Lower floor**
- Bedrooms, baths, laundry, workshop/storage
- Walkout at the waterside via the stepped grade
- Wood soffits (clear finish) carry through under the eave — will pick up warm reflected light

**Site**
- Steep waterfront slope, engineered retaining walls + CIP concrete planter boxes stepping down
- Driveway approach from the road side — "flush wall" detail where siding meets concrete is clean and photogenic
- Waterfront harbour orientation

### What makes this house shootable
- Austere exterior palette (white/neutral vertical siding, dark metal roof) → high-contrast, editorial-friendly
- The gable-end-on-gable-end elevation is almost a pure geometric study
- Central stair volume + glass curtain wall = dramatic interior light
- Skylight roof line pulls natural light deep into the plan
- Cantilever deck over workshop = a defining exterior frame

### What makes this house hard to shoot
- **No landscaping / no furnishings** — this is the listing-prep reality you flagged
- Interiors will read cold/empty without intervention
- No soft layer (plants, textiles, art) to warm up the architecture
- Waterfront harbour side may be where the build shines, but the road side is what buyers approach from

### Shot priorities

**Exterior — what the architecture actually does**
- Hero gable-end elevation (road side) — symmetrical, dead-on, neutral light
- Hero gable-end elevation (water side) — from the water or shoreline, drone at eave-line
- Approach frame from driveway, showing the flush-wall siding-to-concrete detail
- Cantilever deck from below on the waterside, emphasizing the float
- Stepped planter / retaining wall sequence down the slope
- Drone top-down + 30° angle showing the two-wing + linking-volume parti — this is the shot that explains the design

**Interior — the stair volume carries the set**
- Stair-volume frame from the lower floor looking up through the curtain wall — the money shot
- Stair-volume frame from the upper landing looking down
- Kitchen / living open-plan, framed through the deck opening toward water
- Skylight light-cast frames (mid-morning, watch for harsh bars)
- Threshold shots at the wing-to-stair transitions — east wing door, west wing door, both have that detailed trim reveal
- A few ceiling/soffit details where the wood soffit carries indoor to outdoor

**Austere staging — the hard question**
You mentioned maybe staging the foreman's sailboat and possibly AI-furnishing the interiors. My take:

1. **Sailboat at the dock = yes.** It's a scale anchor for the waterfront exteriors and reads as "this is a place that belongs on the water." Coordinate tide.

2. **AI furnishings** — I'd treat this as a post-production add-on, not a shoot-day call. Shoot the space clean, shoot the space honest, then we select 3–5 hero interior frames for AI staging (bed, living seating, dining, kitchen accessory dressing) as a separate deliverable. That way the architectural portfolio version and the listing version are two distinct edits off the same shoot. Happy to quote that as an add-on once we see the empty frames.

3. **Real props we can bring** — one piece of driftwood, a stoneware vase, a linen throw. Small things that give scale without lying about the finish-out.

### Reel beats (15–30s)
- Approach from driveway → pan to reveal gable geometry
- Interior: stair volume light reveal (probably the best single beat)
- Cantilever deck → water view dolly
- Drone pullback at close — house sits in harbour context
- Licensed music, vertical cut — lean Scandi/minimal, not cinematic swell

### Logistics
- Tide chart for waterfront frames and dock staging
- Confirm sailboat is available and berth-ready for the shoot day
- Drone — confirm airspace around Madeira Park harbour
- Clean-up pass needed before shoot day — construction dust, ladder marks on the walkouts, tape residue on glass
- Clear all MEP tags (HRV stickers, window tags) before we arrive
- Blinds / window coverings (if any go in pre-shoot) — confirm
