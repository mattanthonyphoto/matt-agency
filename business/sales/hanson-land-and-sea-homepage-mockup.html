Our History

Three generations of
building on the coast

60s

A Sawmill on Gambier Island

Lex Hanson left Southern California's development industry for something quieter. He built a hexagonal timber frame home on Gambier Island, set up a sawmill, and raised his family surrounded by heavy beams and raw coastal lumber. That hex house became the family homestead and the blueprint for everything that followed.

90s

Marine Roots, Coastal Craft

Marlin and Ryan grew up building alongside their father. Piers, docks, and waterfront structures that demanded durability against the Pacific. The marine DNA stuck: massive beams, simple-yet-strong joinery, and a respect for what the coast can throw at a structure.

"The houses we make reflect where they're from."

Marlin Hanson
10s

Hex Studio to Dwell Magazine

A 250-square-foot hexagonal studio for a birth doula. Hand-split western red cedar shakes, Douglas fir hex flooring, clay plaster walls. Dwell came calling. Then the Roberts Creek Family Home with September Architecture. Then the Airstream Ryan found rusting on a job site. Four features in a decade. Not because they chase press, but because the work speaks.

Now

Two Brothers, Two Regions

Marlin runs the Sunshine Coast from Roberts Creek to Egmont. Ryan leads Powell River and the surrounding islands. Between them: 16 named projects, architect partnerships with Battersby Howat and September Architecture, and a crew that builds like the coast taught them.

"Great project and cost management, good communication throughout, and a highly professional team of craftsmen that really care about their work."

Chris Battson
Custom Home, Sunshine Coast
