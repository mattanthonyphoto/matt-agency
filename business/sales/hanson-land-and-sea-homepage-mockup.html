About Hanson Land & Sea

Rooted in the coast.
Built to endure.

In the late 1960s, Lex Hanson left Southern California for Gambier Island, where he built a hexagonal timber frame home and raised his family among heavy beams and raw coastal lumber.

Today his sons Ryan and Marlin lead Hanson Land & Sea across two regions of the Sunshine Coast, building custom homes with the same principles their father taught them: native materials, durable joinery, and deep respect for the land.

1960s

A Sawmill on
Gambier Island

Lex Hanson left Southern California's development industry for Gambier Island, where he built a hexagonal timber frame home, set up a sawmill, and raised his family among heavy beams and raw coastal lumber.

That hex house became the family homestead and the blueprint for everything that followed.

1990s

Marine Roots,
Coastal Craft

Marlin and Ryan grew up building alongside their father. Piers, docks, and waterfront structures that demanded durability against the Pacific. The marine DNA stuck: massive beams, simple-yet-strong joinery, and a respect for what the coast can throw at a structure.

The houses we make reflect where they're from.

Marlin Hanson
2010s

From Hex Studio
to Dwell Magazine

A 250-square-foot hexagonal studio. Hand-split western red cedar shakes, Douglas fir hex flooring, clay plaster walls. Dwell came calling. Then the Roberts Creek Family Home with September Architecture. Then the Airstream Ryan found rusting on a job site.

Four features in a decade. Not because they chase press, but because the work speaks.

Now

Two Brothers,
Two Regions

Marlin runs the Sunshine Coast from Roberts Creek to Egmont. Ryan leads Powell River and the surrounding islands. Between them: 16 named projects, architect partnerships with Battersby Howat and September Architecture, and a crew that builds like the coast taught them.

What We Build

Five Disciplines,
One Standard

From a 250-square-foot hexagonal studio to a multi-million-dollar oceanfront home, the same commitment to craft and native materials.

01

Custom Homes

Design-build homes shaped by their site. From remote island retreats to architect-designed oceanfront residences, built from local timber with lasting craftsmanship.

02

General Contracting

Full-service project management with transparent budgets, regular reviews, and skilled trades coordination that keeps complex builds on track.

03

Major Renovations

Additions, extensions, and full home transformations that respect the existing structure while reimagining how you live in your space.

04

Commercial

New builds, tenant improvements, and developments. The same quality standards and personal approach, scaled for commercial requirements.

05

Outdoor Structures

Saunas, studios, bunkhouses, and decks built for the coast. Ask us about the Hex Studio that landed in Dwell Magazine.

06

Sustainable Building

Rainwater harvesting, green roofs, reclaimed timber, and energy-efficient design. Sustainability built into the process, not bolted on after.
