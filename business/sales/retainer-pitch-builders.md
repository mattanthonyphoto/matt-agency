# Builder Retainer Pitch — The Trust Ladder

*How to move builders from one-off shoots to ongoing Creative Partner relationships*

---

## The Path: One-Off → Retainer

### Stage 1: First Completed Project Shoot ($3,500)
The audition. Prove you understand their craft, show up prepared, deliver images that make their work look as good as it is, turn around fast. Goal isn't revenue — it's becoming their go-to.

### Stage 2: Construction Progress on Next Build ($1K-$1.5K/visit)
The gateway drug. Monthly visits create dependency and steady recurring revenue disguised as project work.

**Why progress photography is the entry point:**
- Low commitment per visit, but visits happen monthly over 8-18 months
- Creates a story worth 10x a single shoot (months of social content)
- Builders see immediate value in client update images
- Locks in the completed shoot — not bringing someone else in after 12 months
- The pitch: "Let me document your next build from ground-breaking to move-in. Monthly visits, images within 48 hours for client updates and social posts."

### Stage 3: Second Completed Project (Repeat Booking)
Plant the seed: "Most builders I work with end up shooting 4-6 projects a year. At that volume, a retainer saves money and guarantees I'm available when you need me."

### Stage 4: The Retainer Conversation
Frame as: "You're already spending this much with me. Let me formalize it so you get more for less, and I can plan my schedule around your build calendar."

## The Math That Sells It

### A La Carte vs. Retainer

| Service | One-Off Price | Annual (typical builder, 3+ projects) |
|---|---|---|
| 3 completed project shoots | $3,500 each | $10,500 |
| 6 construction progress visits | $1,250 avg each | $7,500 |
| Award submission prep | $500-800/project | $1,500 |
| Team headshots | $750 | $750 |
| **Total a la carte** | | **$20,250** |

**Pilot Retainer:** $2,500/mo × 12 = $30,000 — but includes MORE than above (social content, priority scheduling, drone, content strategy).

**The reframe:** "You're not paying $2,500/month. You're paying $2,500 instead of the $1,700+ you'd spend anyway — and getting twice the deliverables plus priority access."

For builders doing 1-2 projects/year: Pilot at 3 months ($7,500 total) covers one project cycle. Let them experience the difference before committing.

## Value a Retainer Delivers That One-Offs Don't

**For the builder:**
- **Never miss a project.** Every home documented. No more "forgot to book a photographer" on the best build of the year.
- **Always have fresh content.** Instagram, website, proposals always have current work.
- **Consistent visual brand.** Same photographer = cohesive portfolio. Different photographers = fragmented brand.
- **Award pipeline on autopilot.** Every project shot to award-ready standards.
- **Priority scheduling.** First pick of dates. No competing with one-offs during peak season.
- **Cost-share revenue.** With 90% cost-share hit rate, licensing from designers/architects effectively subsidizes the arrangement.
- **Embedded creative partner.** Over time, photographer anticipates needs rather than reacting to briefs.

**For Matt:**
- Predictable revenue ($2,500-$3,000/mo locked in)
- Efficient scheduling (plan around build calendars)
- Portfolio curation (shoot everything, curate the best)
- Deeper relationships = more referrals

## Handling Retainer Objections

**"We don't build enough to justify a retainer."**
"That's actually when it matters most. If you're building 2-3 homes a year, every single one needs to be documented. You can't afford to waste a project. The retainer ensures every build becomes a portfolio piece, social campaign, and potential award submission."

**"What if we don't have anything to shoot some months?"**
"It's not use-it-or-lose-it. We plan the year around your build calendar. Some months we're on-site weekly. Others we're doing team photos or planning the next shoot. The monthly fee smooths out unpredictable costs."

**"Can't we just book you when we need you?"**
"You can. But May through September, I'm typically booked 3-4 weeks out. Retainer clients get priority scheduling — guaranteed availability when a project completes and the light is perfect."

**"We've never paid a monthly fee for photography."**
"You've also never had a photographer who knows your projects from foundation to finishing. Most builders start with one shoot and realize they need ongoing coverage. The retainer just formalizes what already happens naturally."
