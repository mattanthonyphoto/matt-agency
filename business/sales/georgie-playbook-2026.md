## Phase 2: At the Event (May 23)

### Timing & Positioning
- **Arrive early.** Cocktail reception before dinner is the best networking window.
- Position near the bar or appetizer stations — natural congregation points.
- At dinner, don't sit with people you already know. Choose a table with target builders.

### Who to Target (Priority Order)

1. **Custom home builder finalists** — already value how their projects are perceived
2. **Interior designers and architects** — they drive photographer selection. Their recommendation to a builder is the most powerful referral.
3. **Finalists who didn't win** — slightly deflated, looking for ways to strengthen next year
4. **Builders who attended but didn't enter** — watching competitors get recognized, thinking "we should enter next year"

### Conversation Frameworks

**For Winners:**
> "Congratulations on [category] for [project name]. That project is incredible."

Then ask: "What was the most challenging part of the build?" or "How did you land on [specific design choice]?" Let them talk about their craft.

If they ask what you do: "I'm an architectural photographer. I work with custom builders to make sure their best work gets the visibility it deserves — whether for their website, social, or awards like this."

If it goes well: "I'd love to see [project] in person sometime. Would you be open to connecting after the event?"

**For Finalists Who Didn't Win:**
> "I was really impressed by [project name]. The [specific detail] stood out."

Do NOT say "you should have won." Validate the work: "Being a finalist in a field like this is a serious achievement."

If they express frustration: "The judging process is tough — out-of-province judges working from submissions. The photography and presentation can make a huge difference in how the project reads to someone who hasn't walked through it."

**For Non-Entrants:**
> "Great to see you here. Do you typically enter the Georgies, or is this more of a networking night?"

If photography came up as a barrier: "That's one of the things I help builders with. Getting the project documented properly makes the whole submission process easier."

**For Designers/Architects:**
> "I keep seeing [firm name] credited on the finalist entries. You've clearly had an incredible year. Which project are you most proud of?"

If they ask what you do: "I shoot the finished product. I work with builders and designers to make sure the photography matches the level of craft that went into the space."

### Conversation Targets

**Aim for 10-15 meaningful conversations.** Not 30 superficial ones.

A "meaningful conversation" = you learned their name/company/something about their work, they learned your name/what you do, and there's a natural reason to follow up.

### What You're Trying to Get

You're NOT closing deals. You want a reason to follow up.

**Best outcomes:**
1. They suggest meeting for coffee or a site visit (jackpot)
2. You exchange Instagram handles and they follow you on the spot
3. Business card exchange with a specific follow-up reason
4. Name and company clearly enough to find them afterward

**The ask, when natural:** "Would it be alright if I reached out next week? I'd love to learn more about what you have coming up this summer."

Or simpler: "Are you on Instagram? Let me find you."

### What NOT to Do

- Hand out cards like flyers
- Pitch unprompted
- Pull out iPad unless asked
- Drink more than 2
- Spend all night with one person (gracefully exit: "I don't want to keep you — let's definitely connect next week")
- Talk pricing (if asked: "Depends on scope — happy to put something together after we chat")
- Badmouth other photographers
- Bring up cold emails you may have sent them

## Phase 3: The 48-Hour Blitz (May 24-25)

### Sunday Morning (May 24): Organize

Categorize every contact:

**A-List (follow up same day):** Expressed direct interest, suggested meeting, strongest conversations

**B-List (within 48 hours):** Good conversations, mutual interest, no specific next step

**C-List (within the week):** Brief exchanges, cards traded, nothing deep

### Follow-Up Templates

**Channel priority:** Instagram DM first → email if you have it → text only if they gave their number.

**Template 1: Winners**
> Hey [Name], great meeting you at the gala last night. Congratulations again on [project name] — well deserved.
>
> I really enjoyed hearing about [specific thing they told you].
>
> If you ever want a second set of eyes on how your project photography could support your next submission or your marketing, happy to chat. No pressure — I just think what you're building is worth seeing.
>
> Enjoy the win. Talk soon.
> Matt

**Template 2: Finalists Who Didn't Win**
> Hey [Name], great connecting at the Georgies. [Project name] really stood out — the [specific detail] is the kind of work that deserves to be seen more widely.
>
> I know the judging process is tough, and presentation matters a lot in how a project reads on paper. If you're thinking about entering again next year, I'd love to chat about how we could make the photography work harder for you.
>
> Either way, glad we connected. Coffee sometime?
> Matt

**Template 3: Designers/Architects**
> Hey [Name], great talking at the Georgies. I keep seeing [firm] credited across the finalist list — the builders you work with are doing serious work.
>
> I'd love to stay connected. I work with builders in the custom home space and I know how much the photography matters to the design team involved. If you ever have a project wrapping up that deserves proper documentation, happy to chat about collaborating.
> Matt

**Template 4: Non-Entrants**
> Hey [Name], good meeting you at the gala. Sounds like you have some exciting projects on the go.
>
> If you're thinking about entering the Georgies next year, the submission window usually opens around August. Getting the photography done during the build or right at completion makes the whole process smoother.
>
> Happy to chat anytime. Great connecting.
> Matt

## Phase 4: The Longer Play (June-September)

### Converting to Summer Bookings

- **Late May/June:** Discovery calls with warm gala leads
- **June/July:** Shoot completing projects (many builders time for summer)
- **July/August:** Peak exterior conditions (long golden hours, green landscaping)
- **September:** Last strong shooting window before fall

**The pitch:** "You just got recognized at the Georgies — this is the time to invest in imagery that matches your work. Let's document your next project while conditions are ideal."

### The 2027 Georgie Cycle

Based on 2026 timeline:
- **Entry portal opens:** ~August 2026
- **Entry deadline:** ~November 2026
- **Eligible projects:** Likely Jan 1, 2025 - Oct 31, 2026

**Any project shot between now and October 2026 is eligible for 2027 Georgies.**

**The pitch:** "If we photograph this project this summer, you'll have images ready for your 2027 Georgie submission. The entry portal opens in August — builders with photography done in advance have a huge advantage."

### Photography Requirements for Georgie Entries
- All images must be anonymous (no people unless required)
- No black and white or grayscale
- No renderings, virtual staging, or digitally enhanced images
- High-quality, full-color professional photography essentially mandatory to be competitive
- "Photographs are the only way judges can see your project"

### Post-Gala Content
- LinkedIn/blog post: "What I learned at the 2026 Georgie Awards about how BC's best builders present their work"
- Tag builders and designers you met (with context)
- Positions you as embedded in the awards ecosystem
