Website Audit

Professional build.
Leaking leads.

The Squarespace site looks professional and communicates your services clearly. But several critical issues are costing you discovery, trust, and conversions.

Critical Issues
✗
Meta Description Is Blank
Google is generating its own snippet for your homepage instead of showing your value prop. This kills click-through rate from search results. A 155-character description targeting "Victoria BC interior design, building design & pre-construction planning" would fix this instantly.
✗
About Page Returns 404
The standalone About page is broken. People want to know who they're hiring — especially for a service this personal. A broken About page signals neglect. The content exists on the homepage but the dedicated URL is dead.
✗
Building Design Service Page Returns 404
One of your three core service pages is broken. This is a revenue page. Anyone clicking "Building Design" from your navigation hits a dead end. Fix immediately.
✗
Zero Testimonials Anywhere on the Site
Social proof is the #1 conversion driver for design firms. You have a 5-star Houzz review praising Ainsley's work. That quote — and others like it — should be on the homepage, on every service page, and on a dedicated testimonials page.
✗
Site Last Updated November 2024
A site that hasn't been touched in 5+ months signals inactivity to both Google and prospects. Fresh content, new project posts, and blog articles tell Google the site is alive.
Conversion Gaps
!
No Lead Magnet or Email Capture
The only CTA is "Book a Free Discovery Call." That's a big commitment for someone just browsing. A free PDF like "The 10 Decisions to Make Before Your Victoria Renovation" would capture emails from people who aren't ready to call yet.
!
No Case Studies — Just Photos
Portfolio shows what you did but not why or how. Before/after stories with budget ranges, timelines, and challenges overcome convert browsers into callers. "This Gordon Head kitchen was $45K, completed in 8 weeks" tells a story a photo gallery can't.
!
No Blog or Content Strategy
Zero indexable content beyond service pages. No way to rank for high-intent searches like "how much does a kitchen reno cost in Victoria," "garden suite permits Victoria BC," or "do I need a designer before hiring a builder."
!
No FAQ Section
"Do I need a designer for a permit?" "What's included in a Prep & Plan?" These are high-intent Google searches that could drive qualified traffic. They also reduce friction for visitors already on the site.
SEO Gaps
✗
No Structured Data (Schema.org)
Only basic WebSite schema. No LocalBusiness, Service, or Review schema. Google doesn't know DDC is an interior design studio, where you're based, or what services you offer.
!
No Location-Specific Pages
You serve Oak Bay, Gordon Head, Sidney, Sooke, and Pender Island. Each could be a landing page targeting "interior designer [location]" searches. These are low-competition, high-intent keywords.

Website Bottom Line

The site's design quality is solid. The critical fixes are: repair the two broken pages (Building Design + About), add testimonials, and fill in meta descriptions. These three moves alone would significantly improve both trust and search visibility. The content strategy (blog, case studies, lead magnet) builds on that foundation.

Business Model Analysis

Revenue gaps.
Untapped channels.

Your service offering is strong. These are the structural gaps that limit growth and the revenue streams that are sitting on the table.

Pricing & Packaging
!
$125 Consultation Is Underpriced
Standard for a credentialed designer in Victoria is $250–$350. At $125, you're leaving money on the table and potentially signaling a lower tier than your work warrants. Clients willing to pay for design aren't shopping by consultation price.
!
No Tiered Service Packages on Site
Prospects don't know if they're looking at a $5K or $50K engagement. Three clear tiers — Planning Only ($1,500–$3K), Design Package ($5K–$15K), Full Service ($15K–$40K+) — would pre-qualify leads and reduce sticker shock on discovery calls.
Revenue Concentration
!
Builder Referral Channel Is Unsystematized
50% of revenue from builders, but no formal referral program, no reciprocal agreements, no "Preferred Builder" list on the site (which would attract builders who want to be listed). This channel is too important to leave informal.
!
No Recurring Revenue
Every engagement is project-based. Consider a retainer model for builders who send you 3+ clients per year, or a "design concierge" subscription for past clients doing ongoing updates to their homes.
Untapped Revenue Streams
✗
Garden Suite / ADU Consulting
Victoria's new SSMUH framework is creating massive demand for garden suite design. You already do this work, but it's not marketed as a standalone offering. A "Garden Suite Design Package" at a fixed price could be a volume product.
!
Realtor Partnerships
Victoria realtors selling fixer-uppers need someone to tell their buyers "here's what this renovation will actually cost." A co-branded "New Homeowner Renovation Guide" with 2–3 realtors would create a steady referral channel.
!
Awards Pipeline = Zero
No award submissions visible. NKBA, Georgie (CHBA), and local design awards would build credibility and separate you from the pack. Every award entry needs professional photography — which also elevates the portfolio.

Business Model Bottom Line

The foundation is solid. The gaps are packaging (make the tiers visible), partnerships (formalize the builder channel, add realtors), and productizing (garden suite package, awards pipeline). None of this requires hiring — it requires systemizing what you already do.
