# Architect Retainer Model

*Publication-focused retainer for residential architecture firms completing 4-8 projects/year*

---

## How It Differs From the Builder Retainer

| | Builder (Creative Partner) | Architect Retainer |
|---|---|---|
| **Primary goal** | Marketing + sales enablement | Publication + legacy documentation |
| **Content focus** | Lifestyle, process, team, brand | Design intent, spatial quality, material narrative |
| **Beyond photography** | Website, SEO, social, brand strategy | Awards submissions, publication pitching, portfolio curation |
| **Image style** | Warm, aspirational | Clean, precise, design-forward |
| **Usage** | Website, social, ads, sales decks | Awards, publications, portfolio, lectures |
| **Relationship** | Embedded creative department | Trusted visual documentarian |

## The Trust Ladder (Same as Builders, Different Flavor)

### Stage 1: Cost-Share Entry
You're already shooting for a builder. Offer the architect a license through cost-share. They get professional images at $1,200-$1,500 instead of $3,500. Zero risk for them.

### Stage 2: First Direct Commission
They liked the cost-share images. Now they bring you on directly for their next project where the builder isn't coordinating. This is the audition.

### Stage 3: Repeat + Awards
Second or third project together. You help them win an award or get published. The value is now proven.

### Stage 4: The Retainer Conversation
"You're already working with me on most of your projects. Let me formalize it — you get priority scheduling, cost-share coordination on every project, and I handle your awards submissions."

## What Makes the Architect Retainer Unique

### Publication Submission Service
The single most compelling add-on. Most small firms know they should submit but don't have time or knowledge. A photographer who handles the full process — image selection, writing support, editor relationships, file prep — provides enormous value.

No photographer in BC offers this explicitly. Competitive moat.

### Awards Package Preparation
- RAIC Awards of Excellence: up to 30 images
- Canadian Architect Photo Awards: Matt can enter directly
- AFBC Awards: professional photography required
- Georgie Awards: professional photography mandatory for judging

A retainer that includes curated, submission-ready packages for 2-3 programs/year is a tangible deliverable.

### Construction-Phase Documentation
Milestone visits during construction serve two purposes:
1. Compelling before/during/after narratives for publications and social
2. Deeper photographer understanding of the project → better final shoot

Rare in residential photography. Significant differentiator.

### Social Media Content Drops
Quarterly batches of 6-8 images (crops, details, process shots) formatted for Instagram/LinkedIn. Removes the friction architects feel about posting.

### Portfolio Curation
Annual session reviewing the firm's full portfolio — project selection, image sequencing, presentation guidance for website and pitches. Positions Matt as strategic advisor, not just service provider.

- Retainer needs to feel like smart financial planning, not additional expense
- Cost-share coordination means effective net cost to architect is significantly lower
- One feature in Western Living delivers marketing value exceeding the annual retainer
- $2,000/month tier is the sweet spot for initial conversations (below $25K/year psychological threshold)

## When NOT to Pitch the Retainer

- First meeting or first project — too early
- Before proving publication/awards value — need credibility
- To firms completing fewer than 3 projects/year — math doesn't work
- When they're in a cash crunch — bad timing

**The right time:** After 2-3 successful project shoots where the value is proven and the relationship is established. Same Balmoral progression: project → repeat → retainer.
