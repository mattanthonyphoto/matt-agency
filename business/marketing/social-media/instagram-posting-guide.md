# Instagram Posting System — Matt Anthony Photography

## How This Works

1. Open this guide on your phone or laptop
2. Find today's post
3. Copy the caption
4. Open Instagram, create a post
5. Select images in the exact order listed (for carousels)
6. Paste caption, post
7. Check the box when done

**Collab posts:** For any post tagged with a builder/architect, use Instagram's "Tag people" > "Invite collaborator" feature. The post appears on BOTH profiles.

**Caption:**

How do you build a home when your site drops 20 feet to a rocky shoreline?

The Perch demonstrates five strategies for dramatic coastal sites.

Builder: @summerhillfinehomes
Architect: @atelier_michel_laflamme

1. Cantilever over obstacles. The dark wood volume extends beyond the rocky grade, creating space while preserving the shoreline below.

2. Use glass as a bridge. Floor-to-ceiling windows dissolve the boundary between the suspended fireplace inside and the forest canopy outside.

3. Create pavilions, not walls. Transparent living spaces float above the landscape rather than fighting against it.

4. Follow the natural grade. Concrete steps work with existing rock formations, creating seamless transitions.

5. Frame views from private spaces. Even the bathroom becomes a viewing pavilion, with a freestanding tub positioned to capture morning light over the water.

Which strategy would you apply to your most challenging site?
