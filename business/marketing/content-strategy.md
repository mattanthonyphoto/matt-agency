## Content Pillars

### 1. Project Showcases
The portfolio in motion. Final images from completed shoots presented as reveals.
- Instagram carousels (8-10 images per project)
- LinkedIn document carousels (PDF upload — 2-3x more dwell time than images)
- Pinterest pins (multiple pins per project, each linking to portfolio page)
- Journal posts (long-form behind the scenes)

### 2. Process & Behind the Scenes
Show how the work gets made. Builds trust and differentiates from competitors.
- Setup shots, gear in the field, before/after staging
- Stories and Reels from shoot days (8-30 seconds, raw over polished)
- "How I shot this" breakdowns (carousel or Reel format)
- Retouching timelapses (screen capture sped up with music)

### 3. Client Spotlights
Elevate the people you work with. This is the retainer pitch in action.
- Tag clients, share their wins (award nominations, project completions)
- Testimonial graphics
- Case study summaries
- Collaboration stories framed for LinkedIn (B2B angle — how documentation helped their business)

### 4. Educational
Position as the authority on architectural photography in BC.
- Why professional photos matter for builders
- How to prepare for a shoot
- Award submission photography tips
- Seasonal shooting considerations in BC
- "Real estate photography vs. architectural photography" (evergreen differentiator)
- Photography ROI content — stats, case studies, cost comparisons

### 5. Personal / Brand
Let people know who's behind the camera.
- Squamish lifestyle, why this work matters
- Milestone posts (projects completed, years in business)
- Keep rare — max 1-2/month

## Platform Rules

### Instagram (Portfolio + Reach)
- Grid aesthetic matters — alternate between wide/detail, light/dark
- **Carousels dominate:** 10% engagement rate vs. 7% for singles, 1.4x wider reach
- Carousels: 6-13 slides, 4:5 vertical (1080x1350px), repeat first image as last slide
- Reels: 8-30 seconds, process-focused, no talking head required. Raw > polished.
- Stories: shoot day BTS, polls, casual content. Daily when active.
- **Post 4-5x/week:** 2 carousels, 2 Reels, 1 static or Story highlight

### LinkedIn (Primary B2B Acquisition)
- **Post from personal profile** — 561% more reach than company page
- **No links in post body** — put links in first comment (6x more engagement)
- **Document/carousel posts (PDF uploads)** outperform everything else — 2-3x dwell time
- Text-only posts outperform single images by 30%
- First 60 minutes are critical — respond to all comments within 15 minutes
- Longer captions, B2B angle, tag client companies and collaborators
- **Post 4x/week** — see `linkedin-b2b-playbook.md` for full schedule
- 3-5 hashtags max, woven into text

### Pinterest (Passive Traffic Engine)
- Functions as a **visual search engine** — not social media. Every pin links to your website.
- Drives **5x more website traffic** than other social platforms
- **Board names must be search-friendly:** "West Coast Modern Homes" not "Inspiration"
- Pin descriptions: 100-200 words with natural keyword inclusion
- Pin titles: keyword-first, descriptive
- Alt text on every pin — describe the image with keywords
- **Pin 3-5x/day** using scheduling tool (Tailwind or Buffer)
- Create multiple pins from one project — different images to different boards
- Architecture/design is a top-performing niche
- Rich Pins enabled via business account

### Website Journal
- 1-2 posts/month
- SEO-focused: target keywords builders and architects search
- Link to relevant service pages and project pages
- Longer form (800-1500 words)
- Every journal post feeds 3-5 Pinterest pins

## Repurposing Chain

```
Shoot → Final Images
  → Instagram carousel (same week — hero images, project story)
  → Instagram Reel (same week — BTS, before/after reveal, detail montage)
  → LinkedIn document carousel (same week — B2B angle, project story)
  → Pinterest pins (same week — 5-8 individual images across boards)
  → Instagram Stories (shoot day — raw BTS, polls, behind the scenes)
  → Client tag post (week 2 — designed for client to reshare)
  → Educational post (week 2-3 — "why this angle works" or process breakdown)
  → Detail/material close-up posts (week 3-4 — individual shots with micro-captions)
  → Journal post (2-4 weeks later — long-form behind the scenes, SEO-optimized)
  → Case study (if strong project — for proposals and LinkedIn)
  → Testimonial overlay graphic (when testimonial received)
  → Throwback re-share (3-6 months later — seasonal or anniversary tie-in)
  → Proposal insert (ongoing)
  → Award submission collateral (seasonal)
```

**One shoot feeds content for 3-6 months. Minimum 15 pieces of content per project across all platforms.**

### Content Batching Process

1. **After delivery:** Export web-sized versions + social crops (4:5 IG, 2:3 Pinterest, 1.91:1 LinkedIn)
2. **Batch session (2-3 hours):** Write all captions for all platforms using hooks-scripts-library.md templates
3. **Schedule everything:** Load into scheduling tool with dates mapped to the repurposing chain above
4. **Daily (10 min):** Respond to comments, engage with client/prospect posts, post Stories if shooting

## Video / Reels Strategy

### Formats That Perform

| Format | Length | Description |
|--------|--------|-------------|
| Before/After Reveal | 8-15 sec | Construction → final photo. Beat-drop audio on transition. |
| Retouching Timelapse | 15-30 sec | Screen-capture editing process, speed up, add music. |
| Shoot Day BTS | 15-30 sec | Raw footage on location. Ladder, lights, waiting for clouds. |
| Project Walkthrough | 30-60 sec | Guided tour of finished space. Drone-to-interior transition. |
| What I See vs. Camera | 8-15 sec | Split screen: raw scene vs. composed shot. |
| Detail Montage | 15-20 sec | Quick cuts of materials, textures, joinery, light patterns. |
| Drone Reveal | 15-30 sec | Wide establishing shot revealing the full project. |
| Mini Education | 30-60 sec | Quick tip on why certain design choices photograph better. |

### Production Rules
- **Hook in first 2 seconds** — text overlay + movement
- **Raw > polished** — over-produced content loses to authentic footage
- **8-19 seconds** is the sweet spot for completion rate
- Have assistants capture BTS footage during shoots — dedicated phone footage folder
- One project = 3-5 different Reels minimum
- Trending audio helps but isn't required — architectural content often works better with ambient/music
