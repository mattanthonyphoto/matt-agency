# LinkedIn B2B Playbook

LinkedIn is your primary client acquisition channel. Builders, developers, architects, and their marketing teams are all on this platform. Most architectural photographers ignore LinkedIn entirely — that's your edge.

---

## Why LinkedIn Over Everything for B2B

- Personal profile posts get **561% more reach** than company pages
- Linkless posts get **6x more engagement** than posts with links
- Document/carousel posts (PDF uploads) get **2-3x more dwell time**
- Far less competition from photographers vs. Instagram
- Decision-makers (principals, PMs, marketing directors) actively scroll LinkedIn
- Organic reach still works without ad spend

## Profile Optimization Checklist

### Headline
Not just "Architectural Photographer." Include what you do for clients.

**Current:** Architectural Photographer
**Better:** Architectural Photographer | I help builders and architects showcase their best work across BC
**Best:** Documenting design intent for builders, architects, and designers across British Columbia

### Banner Image
Your single strongest portfolio image. Wide format, architectural, recognizable quality. Change it quarterly to feature recent work.

### Profile Photo
Professional headshot. Not a logo. Not you with a camera. People connect with people.

### About Section
```
I photograph buildings for a living — but what I actually do is help builders,
architects, and designers show the world what they're capable of.

Based in Squamish, BC, I work across the Sea-to-Sky, Sunshine Coast, Vancouver,

teams behind the work.

My clients use these images for everything: websites, proposals, award
submissions (Georgie Awards, CHBA), social media, and marketing collateral.
One shoot feeds their content pipeline for months.

If your projects deserve better documentation than iPhone photos, I'd love to
talk about what that looks like.

→ mattanthonyphoto.com
→ info@mattanthonyphoto.com
```

### Featured Section
Pin 3-4 items:
1. Your strongest case study (Summerhill, Balmoral, or Sitelines)
2. A journal post with strong visuals
3. A LinkedIn carousel post that performed well
4. Your website homepage or portfolio

### Recommendations
Request recommendations from 3-5 past clients. These act as professional references visible to anyone who visits your profile. Ask specifically:
- "Would you write a quick recommendation about working together on [project]?"
- Guide them: "A sentence about the experience and a sentence about the results would be perfect."

### Video Introduction
Record a 30-second video (LinkedIn allows this as a secondary profile element):
- Who you are, what you do, who you do it for
- Casual, not scripted — same energy as meeting someone at a networking event

## Content Strategy

### Posting Cadence
**4x per week** from your personal profile.

| Day | Content Type | Pillar |
|-----|-------------|--------|
| Monday | Project carousel (PDF document) | Showcase |
| Tuesday | Industry insight or educational text post | Educational |
| Thursday | Client spotlight or collaboration story | Client Spotlight |
| Friday | BTS, personal reflection, or contrarian take | Process / Personal |

### Content Types Ranked by LinkedIn Performance

1. **Document/Carousel Posts (PDF uploads)** — Highest dwell time. Upload a PDF with one image/point per page. LinkedIn's algorithm rewards time-on-post.
2. **Text-only posts** — Outperform single images by 30%. Use for insights, lessons, industry observations.
3. **Video** — Good for BTS and project reveals. Native upload only (never YouTube links).
4. **Single image + long caption** — Standard but lower reach than text-only or documents.
5. **Posts with external links** — Worst performer. Always put links in the first comment.

### Document Carousel Format

Create PDFs (Canva or equivalent) with this structure:
- **Slide 1:** Hook — bold text, one sentence, project image background
- **Slides 2-6:** One point or image per slide with minimal text
- **Slide 7:** Takeaway or CTA
- **Dimensions:** 1080x1350px (portrait) or 1280x720px (landscape)

**Topics that work as LinkedIn carousels:**
- Project reveal walkthrough (5-8 best shots from a project)
- "How we shot [Project Name]" — BTS process breakdown
- "5 things builders should know about photography for [awards/marketing/proposals]"
- Before/after series (construction → completion)
- "What $X of professional photography gets you" — deliverables breakdown
- Client case study: the problem, the solution, the results

### The First 60 Minutes Rule

LinkedIn's algorithm tests your post in the first 60 minutes. What you do in this window determines whether it gets amplified or buried.

1. **Post at optimal times:** Tuesday-Thursday, 8-10am Pacific
2. **Respond to every comment within 15 minutes** — this generates a 90% algorithmic boost
3. **Don't edit the post** in the first hour — edits reset the algorithm
4. **Engage with 3-5 other people's posts** before and after yours — signals activity

### Caption Structure for LinkedIn

```
[Line 1: Hook — this is all that shows before "see more"]
[Line 2: Second hook line or context — also visible in preview]

[Blank line]

[Body: 3-5 short paragraphs. One thought per line. Liberal use of line
breaks. LinkedIn readers scan, they don't read walls of text.]

[Closing: Insight, reflection, or question — not a hard sell]

[3-5 hashtags woven naturally or at the end]
```

**Formatting that works:**
- Short paragraphs (1-3 sentences max)
- Line breaks between every thought
- Occasional bold emphasis using **Unicode bold text** (LinkedIn doesn't support markdown)
- Numbered lists for tactical content
- Rhetorical questions to drive comments

## Client Acquisition Playbook

### Finding Decision-Makers

Use LinkedIn's search to find:
- **Marketing Directors / Marketing Managers** at target builder/developer firms
- **Principals / Partners** at architecture and design firms
- **Project Managers** at construction companies
- **Business Development** roles at firms you want to work with

Search queries:
- "Marketing Director" + "custom homes" + "British Columbia"
- "Principal" + "architecture" + "Vancouver"
- "Construction" + "Project Manager" + "Sea-to-Sky"

### Connection Strategy

**Never send a blank connection request.** Always include a personalized note (300 character limit):

Templates:
```
Hi [Name] — I came across [Company]'s [specific project or post].
Really impressive work. I photograph architecture and interiors across BC
and would love to connect.
```

```
Hi [Name] — I've been following [Company]'s projects in [area].
Your team's attention to [specific detail] stands out. Would be great
to connect.
```

```
Hi [Name] — Congratulations on [award/project completion/milestone].
I document projects like this across the Sea-to-Sky and Sunshine Coast.
Happy to connect.
```

### The Warm Nurture Sequence

After connecting, don't pitch immediately. Follow this sequence:

1. **Week 1:** Like and comment on one of their posts (genuine, specific comment)
2. **Week 2:** Share one of your project posts that's relevant to their work
3. **Week 3:** Comment on another of their posts with an insight or question
4. **Week 4:** If they've engaged with any of your content, send a direct message:

```
Hey [Name] — I've been enjoying seeing [Company]'s work come through
my feed. I noticed [specific observation about their projects/marketing].

I specialize in architectural and construction photography across BC and
work with a few builders in [their area]. Would you be open to a quick
conversation about how photography fits into your marketing?

No pressure either way — happy to just stay connected.
```

### Daily LinkedIn Routine (10 minutes)

1. **Check notifications** — respond to all comments on your posts (2 min)
2. **Engage with 3-5 posts** from people in your network — architects, builders, developers. Leave meaningful comments, not "Great post!" (5 min)
3. **Send 2-3 connection requests** to new prospects with personalized notes (3 min)

This daily habit compounds. Within 3 months you'll have a warm network of 200+ decision-makers in your target market.

## What NOT to Do on LinkedIn

- Don't post from a company page (561% less reach)
- Don't include links in the post body (6x less engagement — use first comment)
- Don't use more than 5 hashtags (looks spammy, reduces reach)
- Don't post and ghost — the algorithm rewards engagement in the first hour
- Don't use generic comments on others' posts ("Great work!" / "Love this!")
- Don't pitch in connection requests — build the relationship first
- Don't post the same content as Instagram — adapt the angle for a B2B audience
- Don't edit posts within the first hour (resets algorithm)
