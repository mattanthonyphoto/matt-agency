# Pinterest Launch Playbook

Pinterest is a visual search engine, not a social media platform. People come here to search, save, and plan — which means every pin is a long-tail asset that drives website traffic for months or years. Architecture and design are top-performing categories.

---

## Why Pinterest for Architectural Photography

- Drives **5x more website traffic** than other social platforms
- Users are searching with **purchase/hiring intent** (planning a build, looking for inspiration, researching designers)
- Content has an **extremely long shelf life** — pins gain traction over months, not hours
- **570M+ monthly active users**, 1.5B pins saved weekly
- Architecture/design/home are **top-performing categories**
- Almost zero competition from architectural photographers (most ignore Pinterest entirely)

## Pin Optimization

### Pin Image Specs
- **Aspect ratio:** 2:3 vertical (1000x1500px) — takes up the most screen space in the feed
- **Minimum width:** 600px (Pinterest recommends 1000px)
- **File format:** JPG or PNG
- **Text overlay:** Optional. If used, keep minimal — Pinterest's algorithm can read text in images

### Pin Title (crucial for search)
- **Keyword-first, descriptive, specific**
- 100 character max (aim for 60-80)
- Include: style, type, location, standout feature

**Good titles:**
- "West Coast Modern Custom Home in Whistler BC — Timber Frame with Mountain Views"
- "Contemporary Kitchen Design with Waterfall Island — Squamish Custom Home"
- "Floor-to-Ceiling Windows in a Sea-to-Sky Mountain Home — Architectural Photography"
- "Behind the Scenes: Photographing a $2M Custom Home in Pemberton BC"

**Bad titles:**
- "New project!" (no keywords)
- "Beautiful home" (too generic)
- "The Perch" (project name means nothing to searchers)

### Pin Description
- 100-200 words with natural keyword inclusion
- Don't keyword-stuff — write for humans first, search second
- Include: project type, location, key design features, materials, who designed/built it
- End with a CTA: "See the full project at mattanthonyphoto.com"
- Add 5-10 relevant hashtags

**Template:**
```
[Project type] in [Location], British Columbia featuring [2-3 key design
elements — e.g., "floor-to-ceiling glazing, exposed timber beams, and
a cantilevered living space overlooking Howe Sound"]. Designed by
[architect/designer], built by [builder].

[One sentence about what makes this space/detail special — the light,
the material choice, the site response.]

[One sentence connecting to broader design trend or appeal — "A perfect
example of how West Coast Modern architecture responds to its mountain
setting."]

Architectural photography by Matt Anthony Photography, Squamish BC.
See the full project gallery at mattanthonyphoto.com.

#WestCoastModern #CustomHome #ArchitecturalPhotography #BCArchitecture
#[Location] #ResidentialDesign #[MaterialTag] #ModernArchitecture
#HomeDesign #InteriorDesign
```

### Alt Text
Pinterest uses alt text for accessibility AND search ranking. Write it for every pin.

**Formula:** "[Adjective] [room/space type] with [key features] in a [style] [building type] in [Location], BC."

**Example:** "Bright modern living room with floor-to-ceiling windows and exposed timber beams in a West Coast Modern custom home in Whistler, BC."

### Destination URL
**Always link to the specific project page on your website** — not the homepage, not the portfolio landing page. The deeper the link, the better for both SEO and user experience.

## Posting Cadence

### Launch Phase (Month 1-2)
- **5-10 pins per day** to build initial content volume
- Work through your back catalog — every project you've ever shot is fair game
- Spread pins across different boards
- Schedule using Tailwind or Buffer to maintain consistency

### Steady State (Month 3+)
- **3-5 pins per day**
- New project content as it's batched (see content-batching.md)
- Refresh older pins: re-pin to new boards, create alternate pin designs for the same image
- Journal posts generate 3-5 new pins each

### Scheduling
- **Best times:** Evenings (7-11pm) and weekends — when people browse for inspiration
- **Use a scheduler** — Tailwind SmartSchedule or Buffer. Never pin manually throughout the day.
- **Spread pins** across the day — don't dump 5 pins at once

## SEO Strategy

### Keyword Research
Think about what your target audience searches for on Pinterest:
- "modern home design"
- "custom home ideas"
- "west coast architecture"
- "kitchen design inspiration"
- "timber frame homes"
- "mountain house design"
- "[city] custom homes"
- "architectural photography"
- "how to prepare home for photos"

Use Pinterest's search bar autocomplete to discover related terms. Type a keyword and see what Pinterest suggests — those are high-volume searches.

### Where Keywords Go
1. **Profile display name** — "Matt Anthony Photography | Architectural Photography BC"
2. **Profile bio** — natural keyword inclusion
3. **Board names** — keyword phrases, not creative names
4. **Board descriptions** — 2-3 sentences with keywords
5. **Pin titles** — keyword-first, descriptive
6. **Pin descriptions** — natural keyword integration throughout
7. **Pin alt text** — descriptive with keywords
8. **Pin image file names** — rename before uploading: `west-coast-modern-kitchen-whistler-bc.jpg` not `IMG_4392.jpg`

### Avoid
- Keyword stuffing (repeating the same phrase unnaturally)
- Irrelevant hashtags
- Generic board names ("Inspiration," "Pretty Things," "Work")
- Linking to the homepage instead of specific project pages
- Landscape-oriented pins (they get less real estate in the feed)

## Growth Timeline

Set realistic expectations:

| Timeframe | Expected Results |
|-----------|-----------------|
| Month 1 | Account set up, 100+ pins published, boards established. Minimal traffic. |
| Month 2-3 | 5,000-10,000 monthly impressions. Pinterest's algorithm learning your content. |
| Month 3-6 | 10,000-50,000 monthly impressions. Outbound clicks beginning to grow. |
| Month 6-12 | 50,000-200,000 monthly impressions. Consistent website traffic from Pinterest. |
| Year 1+ | Pinterest becomes a passive traffic engine. Old pins continue driving clicks indefinitely. |

**Key insight:** Pinterest is a slow burn. Unlike Instagram (where a post dies in 48 hours), pins gain traction over weeks and months. The work you put in during month 1-3 pays off for years.

## Monthly Pinterest Review

Add to the monthly analytics review:

1. **Top 5 pins by impressions** — what keywords/topics are resonating?
2. **Top 5 pins by outbound clicks** — what's driving actual website traffic?
3. **Board performance** — which boards are growing fastest?
4. **Audience demographics** — are you reaching homeowners, designers, or builders?
5. **Search terms** — what are people searching to find your pins? (Pinterest Analytics → Audience → Search terms)
6. **Actions:** Publish more content in your top-performing categories. Rename underperforming boards with better keywords. Test new pin formats for low performers.
