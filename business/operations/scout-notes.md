## Moment Compositions (Human Element)

> Walk the space and identify 3-5 spots where a human figure would elevate the shot. Think: where would someone naturally pause, sit, look, or move? The architecture is still the hero — the person gives it scale, warmth, and story.

### Common moment types to look for

- **Threshold moments:** Figure in a doorway, at the top of stairs, entering a room. Creates depth and narrative.
- **View moments:** Someone looking out a window, standing on a deck facing the landscape. Connects interior to site.
- **Scale moments:** A person in a double-height space, walking a long corridor, standing beneath a cantilevered roof. Reveals the architecture's ambition.
- **Touch moments:** Hand on a material — wood grain, stone counter, steel railing. Intimate, connects craft to experience.
- **Silhouette moments:** Figure backlit by a large window or glass wall. Dramatic, minimal, magazine-ready.
- **Movement moments:** Someone walking through frame with motion blur (long exposure). Adds life without demanding attention.

### Notes on timing

> Figure work happens AFTER the core architectural shots are complete. Plan it for the last 60-90 minutes when you can experiment without pressure.
