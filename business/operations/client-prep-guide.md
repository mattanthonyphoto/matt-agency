## Before the Shoot

### Cleaning & Maintenance

**Interior:**
- [ ] All floors cleaned (vacuum, mop, polish as appropriate)
- [ ] All countertops and surfaces cleared of clutter
- [ ] Kitchen: clear counters of small appliances, dish racks, soap bottles, sponges. Leave 1-2 intentional items if staged (cutting board, fruit bowl, kettle)
- [ ] Bathrooms: clear all personal products from counters, shower, tub edge. Fresh towels hung (white or neutral preferred)
- [ ] Remove all visible garbage cans / recycling bins
- [ ] Windows cleaned — interior and exterior (this makes a significant difference)
- [ ] Light fixtures / pendants dusted and all bulbs working
- [ ] All interior doors open to their natural resting position
- [ ] Toilet lids down

**Exterior:**
- [ ] Driveway / walkways swept or power-washed
- [ ] Front entry clean and welcoming
- [ ] Lawn mowed, hedges trimmed, garden beds tidy
- [ ] Hoses coiled and stored out of sight
- [ ] Garbage / recycling bins moved out of frame
- [ ] Construction materials, tools, dumpsters removed (if project is complete)
- [ ] Vehicles moved away from the front of the house

### Staging Notes

**General:**
- Less is more. We can always add, but a cluttered space is hard to fix.
- Remove personal items: family photos, mail, charging cables, water bottles, medications
- Remove branded items: Amazon boxes, shopping bags, visible product labels
- Rugs straight, pillows fluffed, throws draped intentionally
- All blinds/shutters open and set to the same position

**If the space is furnished / occupied:**
- We're not expecting a showroom — just a tidy, intentional version of the space
- Kids' toys, pet items, and daily-life clutter tucked away for the shoot
- If the homeowner is present, let them know the photographer will be moving through the space quietly

**If professionally staged:**
- Confirm staging is complete and stylist has approved before shoot day
- Fresh flowers or greenery are a plus (simple arrangements, not overpowering)

### Lighting

- **Turn on all designed lighting** — pendants, sconces, under-cabinet, cove lighting, landscape lights. Every fixture that was part of the design should be on.
- Replace any burned-out bulbs.
- All bulbs in the same fixture should match in color temperature (warm or cool, not mixed).
- Fireplace lit if it's gas (we'll confirm timing on shoot day).

---

## On Shoot Day

### What to Expect

- I'll arrive **30 minutes early** to walk the space and plan compositions.
- A typical completed-project shoot takes **3-4 hours** for interiors and daylight exteriors. Twilight adds 1-2 hours at the end.
- I work methodically through the space, chasing the best natural light. I may rearrange small items (pillows, chairs, objects) and will put everything back.
- I shoot on a tripod with careful compositions — it's not fast, but it's precise.
- If a second shooter is present, they'll be capturing supplementary angles and details.

### Access

- I need full access to every room we're photographing.
- If any rooms are locked, have keys available.
- Alarm codes: please share in advance if applicable.
- Garage: if it's part of the shoot, vehicles should be removed.

### Occupants

- If anyone is home during the shoot, that's fine — I'll work around them.
- I may politely ask people to step out of a room for a few minutes while I shoot it.
- Pets: please keep them in a separate area or outside during the shoot. We love them, but they're hard to Photoshop out of reflections.

### Weather

- Overcast skies are actually ideal for architectural photography — even, diffused light with no harsh shadows.
- Light rain is workable for interiors. We may shift exterior shots to another day if heavy rain.
- If the weather is a concern, I'll reach out the day before to confirm or reschedule.
