Why This Matters

What happens when interiors are photographed like everything else

Most photographers light a room and shoot wide. The result is flat, bright, and interchangeable. Indistinguishable from the last ten portfolios an editor scrolled past.

Your work isn't about brightness. It's about atmosphere, material honesty, and spatial logic. Photography that misses those qualities doesn't just fail to represent your design. It actively undermines it.

Editorial Rejection

Publications evaluate spatial clarity and material legibility. Over-lit, over-edited images signal that the photographer didn't understand the design. Editors move on.

Portfolio That Doesn't Convert

If your imagery looks like every other designer's, prospective clients can't tell why your work is different. The portfolio fills space instead of building reputation.

Design Read as Decoration

When spatial decisions and material relationships aren't captured with intent, your work gets reduced to "pretty rooms." The thinking behind it becomes invisible.

Wasted Investment

A $500 shoot that produces unusable images costs far more than the fee itself. It costs you the editorial feature, the award submission, and the next client who never called.

Restraint Over Spectacle

No over-lighting. No heavy manipulation. No renders, no composites. Spaces are shown as they actually live, with the subtlety and atmosphere your design decisions created.

Atmosphere, Not Decoration

Photography that captures spatial logic, material relationships, and the qualities that earn editorial credibility. Not just social media engagement.

Intent Before Coverage

Every shoot begins with understanding your design decisions. Compositions are shaped by judgment, not by trying to photograph everything in the room.

Common Questions

Before you reach out

How far in advance should I book?

Ideally 2 to 4 weeks before the project is photo-ready. For award or editorial submissions, the earlier the conversation starts the better. Ideally before final staging is complete.

Do I need to have the space styled?

I photograph interiors as they are, whether lived-in or styled. If staging is needed, I'll provide a shoot-prep checklist and can advise on what to keep, remove, or adjust for maximum impact.

How does cost-sharing work?

I coordinate everything. You just need to let me know who else was involved in the project. Or better yet, suggest a shared shoot to the builder or architect yourself. Each party gets their own curated set composed around their specific priorities, with full unlimited usage.

Do you offer video as well?

Yes. Both the Residential Interior and Feature-Ready packages include a social media reel and a project video. The Residential tier includes a 60-second atmospheric walkthrough. The Feature-Ready tier includes a longer editorial film. Everything is captured during the same shoot day.

Can I use the images anywhere I want?

Yes. Every engagement includes a full unlimited use license. Website, social media, print, editorial, advertising, award submissions. No restrictions. They're your images.

What if I want changes after delivery?

The curated gallery is a finished deliverable. If specific adjustments are needed, like colour correction, crop modifications, or additional formats, those are accommodated. Reshoots or significant scope changes are quoted as a separate session.

Do you travel outside the Sea-to-Sky corridor?

Yes. I regularly work across Whistler, Pemberton, the Sunshine Coast, Vancouver, and the Fraser Valley. Travel beyond these areas is accommodated and quoted as part of the proposal.

What's the first step?

Book a discovery call. We'll talk about your project, your design intent, and what you need the images to do. No pricing at that stage. Just understanding whether we're a good fit. If we are, I'll follow up with a proposal.
