# Editing & Visual Style Guide

Standards for anyone editing images under the Matt Anthony Photography brand.

## Philosophy

Document the space as the designer intended it. Natural, honest, intentional. The architecture is the subject — our job is to present it clearly, not reinterpret it.

## Color & Tone

- **Natural color grading** — true to materials. Wood should look like wood, concrete like concrete.
- **Slightly desaturated** — pull back vibrancy ~10-15%. Avoid oversaturation, especially in greens and blues.
- **Warm neutral** — lean warm but never orange. Skin tones (if present) should look natural.
- **No heavy color grading, film presets, or trendy looks.** The style should be timeless.

## Exposure & Light

- **Expose for the room** — natural light is primary. Flash/strobe is supplemental, not dominant.
- **Lift shadows** moderately — show detail in dark areas without going flat.
- **Protect highlights** — preserve window views when possible (window pulls if needed).
- **No HDR look.** If blending exposures, it should be invisible.

## Composition & Geometry

- **Verticals must be straight.** Non-negotiable. Use lens corrections and manual adjustment.
- **No barrel distortion.** Correct in post if the lens introduces any.
- **Clean sightlines** — the eye should travel through the space naturally.
- **Shoot at standing eye height** (~5ft) for interiors unless there's a reason not to.
- **Two-point perspective preferred** for interiors (camera level, two vanishing points).

## Retouching

### Remove
- Construction debris, tools left behind
- Visible cables and cords (unless architectural)
- Staging imperfections (crooked cushions, visible price tags)
- Photographer's reflection in glass/mirrors
- Temporary signage, dumpsters, port-a-potties in exteriors

### Keep
- Material texture and grain (wood, stone, concrete, metal)
- Natural light patterns and shadows
- Intentional design elements (even if they look "imperfect")
- Landscape and environment as-is (don't composite skies)

## File Management

### Naming Convention
```
YYYY-MM_ClientName_ProjectName_###.ext
```
Example: `2026-03_Balmoral_WarblerWay_001.jpg`

### Export Presets

| Use | Format | Long Edge | Quality | Color Space |
|-----|--------|-----------|---------|-------------|
| Web / portfolio | JPEG | 2400px | 85% | sRGB |
| Print / publication | TIFF | Full res | Uncompressed | Adobe RGB |
| Social (feed) | JPEG | 1080x1350 | 90% | sRGB |
| Social (story) | JPEG | 1080x1920 | 90% | sRGB |
| Award submission | Per spec | Full res | 95% | sRGB |

## Drone / Aerial

- Horizon must be perfectly level
- Color-match aerial shots to ground-level set
- Same desaturation and warmth as interior edits
- Include environmental context — don't crop too tight

## Consistency Check

Before delivering any set, review the full gallery in sequence:
- White balance consistent across rooms?
- Exposure consistent across similar spaces?
- Color grading matches between interior and exterior?
- Verticals straight in every frame?
- No missed retouching items?
