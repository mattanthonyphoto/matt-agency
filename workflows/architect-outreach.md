## The Approach (NOT Cold Email)

Architects are peer relationships, not lead-gen targets. The approach is fundamentally different from builder outreach.

### Step 1: Research (Before Any Contact)

For each target architect:
1. Review their full portfolio on their website
2. Identify 2-3 projects you'd genuinely want to photograph
3. Check if any projects are in your service area (Sea-to-Sky, Sunshine Coast)
4. Note which builders they work with (cross-reference with your builder pipeline)
5. Check their Instagram — engagement level, posting frequency, content quality
6. Check if they credit a photographer anywhere

### Step 2: Warm-Up (2-4 Weeks)

**Instagram (for firms with active accounts):**
- Follow the firm account
- Engage with specific, knowledgeable comments on project posts
- Reference architectural details, not generic praise
- Comment on material choices, spatial relationships, site responses
- Example: "The way the Robin Drive house steps down the slope to preserve the tree canopy is really well resolved."

**In-person (for local firms):**
- Sea to Sky CHBA events
- Squamish community events
- Ask for an intro through mutual connections (builders you already work with)

### Step 3: The First Contact

**The pitch is NOT:** "I take great photos of buildings."
**The pitch IS:** "I help you get published and win awards."

**DM/Email template (adapt to each firm):**

> Hi [Name], I'm Matt Anthony, an architectural photographer based here in Squamish. I've been following [firm]'s work, the [specific project] in particular caught my eye.
>
> I specialize in design-intent photography for publication. I deliver images that meet ArchDaily and Dezeen submission requirements, and I handle cost-sharing with the builder and other trades so everyone gets the documentation at a fraction of the individual cost.
>
> If you have a project completing this summer, I'd love to discuss photographing it. Happy to grab a coffee.

**Key elements:**
- Local connection (Squamish-based, same community)
- Specific project reference (proves you looked)
- Publication framing (what they care about)
- Cost-share (removes budget objection from builder side)
- Low-pressure ask (coffee, not a contract)

### Step 4: The Test Shoot

If an architect expresses interest, offer a test shoot or introductory rate on one project:

> "I want to build a relationship with your firm. I'll shoot this project at [reduced rate / editorial-only rate] so you can see how I work. If the images get published, we talk about an ongoing arrangement."

This is an investment in the flywheel, not a discount. One strong set of images for STARK could generate 20+ project opportunities over 2 years.

### Step 5: Cost-Share to Meet the Builder

After shooting the architect's project:
1. Ask: "Who built this? Would the builder benefit from having these images too?"
2. Contact the builder: "I just shot [project] for [architect]. The images are going to [publication]. Would you like a license? Through cost-sharing, it would be $X instead of commissioning a full shoot."
3. The builder now has professional images without organizing or art-directing anything.
4. You now have a direct builder relationship.

### Step 6: The Flywheel Spins

The builder's next project (with a different architect):
- They call you directly because they liked the images
- They know you handle cost-sharing
- They know you can get work published
- The new architect on that project now sees your work
- Cycle repeats

## Publication Pipeline

Architects care about publications. Help them get there.

### Submission Requirements

| Publication | Images | Resolution | Exclusivity | Notes |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| ArchDaily | 15+ minimum | 2880px+ width | No | Largest architecture platform |
| Dezeen | 10+ | High-res | Yes — wants first publication | Must be "fresh, innovative, newsworthy" |
| Canadian Architect | Varies | High-res | No | Photo Awards of Excellence open Aug 2026 |
| Azure Magazine | Varies | High-res | Preferred | Major Canadian design pub |
| Western Living | Varies | High-res | No | BC/Alberta lifestyle + design |

### The Offer

"Every project I shoot for you is delivered publication-ready. I know the specs for ArchDaily, Dezeen, and Canadian Architect. I can prepare your submission package."

This differentiates you from every other photographer who just delivers files.

## Key Principles

1. **Relationship, not transaction.** These are 5-10 year partnerships, not one-off gigs.
2. **Publication is the currency.** Getting their work published is more valuable than any discount.
3. **Cost-share is the bridge.** It connects you to the builder without the architect spending more.
4. **Local advantage is real.** Being in Squamish when STARK is in Squamish matters. Use it.
5. **Patience.** The flywheel takes 6-12 months to start spinning. But once it does, it compounds.
