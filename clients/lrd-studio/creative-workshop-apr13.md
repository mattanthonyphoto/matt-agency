**Date:** Monday, April 13, 2026
**Location:** LRD Studio office, 37758 3rd Avenue, Squamish
**Format:** ~2 hour in-person creative workshop (not a Zoom Q&A)
**Goal:** Leave Lauren's office with voice rules, project rights, visual direction, competitor positioning, photo rights path, and approval workflow — captured on physical artifacts you can photograph and transcribe.

## SEGMENT 1 — The Word Wall (15 min)

**Goal:** Extract voice rules as an artifact, not an answer. Capture her exact vocabulary.

**Setup:** Take out 3 stacks of sticky notes. Gold/yellow, red/pink, and blue.

**Script:**
> "I'm writing blog posts, captions, and website copy in your voice for the next 12 months. I want to get this 100% right. So I'm going to ask you to play a quick game with me. No thinking, no overthinking — just react."

**Exercise 1: Gold Notes (5 min)**

Give her the gold sticky notes and a sharpie.

> "Write down every word you reach for when you describe your work. Like, the words you actually say when you're talking to a client about a design. Ten words minimum. Go."

She writes. When she's done, she reads each aloud and sticks them to the wall in a cluster. **Capture every word on your phone as a photo.**

**Exercise 2: Red Notes (3 min)**

> "Now red. Every word that makes you cringe when you see it on a designer's website. Every cliché, every overused bit of design-speak, every word you would never let someone use to describe LRD. Ten minimum."

Same thing. Sticks to the wall next to the gold.

**Exercise 3: The Complete the Sentence (5 min)**

Hand her 4 blue sticky notes. Read each prompt aloud, she writes her answer on a note:

1. *"Good design tells the story of ______."* (her existing tagline is "who we are" — she might change it)
2. *"LRD Studio is ______."* (one-line positioning)
3. *"We are NOT ______."* (what she actively pushes against)
4. *"If someone says our work feels ______, we've failed."* (the worst compliment)

Photograph the whole wall before you move on. This becomes the voice rules doc.

**Sneaky bonus extraction:** While she's doing this exercise, she's going to talk out loud. Write down any full phrases she says that are NOT on the sticky notes. Those are gold too.

## SEGMENT 4 — Project Dot Vote (20 min)

**Goal:** Walk all 17 projects. Capture rights, priority, and stories. This is the longest and most tactile segment.

**Setup:** Spread the 17 project cards across her big table face-up in a rough grid. Put three piles of dot stickers next to them: green, yellow, red.

**Script:**
> "I'm going to walk you through your whole portfolio. For each project, I need three things: can we publish it, do we feature it prominently or de-emphasize it, and is there a story I should know. I'll make notes, you just tell me."

For each project, ask in this exact order:

1. **PUBLISH?** — "Green dot if we can publish freely, yellow if limited, red if no." She places the dot. *(If red, ask why — NDA, client sensitivity, photography rights, personal preference?)*
2. **FEATURE?** — "How prominently do you want this featured? One of your top 5, middle of the pack, or back-of-the-portfolio?" Write her answer on the card.
3. **STORY?** — "Tell me one thing about this project that's not on the website." This is the gold — these become blog post hooks. Write her exact phrases on the card.

**Do this for all 17. Let it breathe.** Don't rush. She'll get tired around project 10 — take a 60-second stretch, keep going.

**At the end, ask these three synthesis questions:**

1. *"Looking at all these cards, what are your top 5? The ones you'd show a top-tier prospective client first."*
2. *"Any projects NOT on this table that you want added — older work, in-progress, or something we're not tracking?"*
3. *"Any of these that you've already pitched to Western Living, House & Home, Dwell, or any publication? I don't want to double-pitch."*

**Photograph every card before you leave.**

## SEGMENT 7 — The Sunstone Question (10 min)

**Goal:** Resolve photo rights. This blocks the biggest asset in the plan.

**Setup:** Put away the visual identity pages. Pull out a blank index card.

**Script:**
> "Last big one. Sunstone is our biggest strategic asset — it's the fresh award, it's Pemberton where no one's defending the SERP, and the search results are literal whitespace. I want to build a dedicated case study page and launch the first blog post around it. But Darby shot Sunstone. Before I plan anything around those images, I need to understand where we stand with her."

Ask these one at a time, pause for real answers:

1. *"What's your current usage agreement with Darby? Full rights? Limited? Project-specific?"*
2. *"Have you already used any of the Sunstone photos on the site? What did you license?"*
3. *"What's your relationship with Darby like — close, cordial, complicated?"*
4. *"How do you feel about emailing her this week for expanded rights — web, blog, SEO use for 12 months?"*
5. *"If she doesn't grant that or quotes us too high, are you open to me re-shooting Sunstone as one of our Q2 or Q3 project shoots?"*

**Write the decided path on the index card in front of her (so it feels committal):**

```
SUNSTONE PHOTO PATH:

□ Full rights already held → proceed with original plan
□ Lauren emails Darby this week for expanded rights
□ We budget $______ to license expanded rights
```

## SEGMENT 8 — Content Pillars (15 min)

**Goal:** Confirm or adjust the content pillar framework that drives every piece of content for 12 months.

**Setup:** Open the Content Pillars handout (08) on the laptop. Walk her through the four-quadrant grid.

**Script:**
> "Everything we post for LRD maps to one of four pillars. I've built a default mix based on what works best for interior designers at your stage — 50% finished project work, 25% material and detail shots, 15% behind-the-scenes and process, and 10% awards and press. Each pillar has a different job. Project Showcase drives saves and inquiries. Material and Detail dominates Pinterest and pulls long-tail search. Process and BTS builds the human connection — the parasocial bond that makes people feel like they know you. Awards and Press is the social proof that closes."

**Walk each pillar. For each one, ask:**

1. *"Does this feel like the right weight? Would you give more to one and less to another?"*
2. *"Are you comfortable with BTS content? Things like you on-site, your sketchbook, sourcing trips? Or would you rather stick to finished work only?"*
3. *"Which projects do you want featured the MOST in the next 3 months? Which ones should stay quieter?"*
4. *"Are there any collaborators you can't publicly tag? Photographer conflicts, builder relationships, client NDAs?"*
5. *"The weekly cadence is Monday-Wednesday-Friday: Project / Material or BTS / Project or Press. Does that rhythm work for you?"*

**If she wants to adjust the sliders:** Use the range sliders in the fillable handout. Capture the final percentages.

**Capture her exact words when she talks about BTS comfort.** This determines how much of the Process pillar you can actually execute. If she's not comfortable showing unfinished work or personal moments, that 15% gets redistributed to Material/Detail.

## SEGMENT 9 — The 12-Month Editorial Calendar (10 min)

- *"Which 3 of these 12 are you most excited about?"*

## CONTINGENCIES

### If she's in a bad mood / distracted when you arrive
