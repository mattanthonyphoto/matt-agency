There's a round stone structure in the middle of this house. Everything revolves around it. Floating wood treads and glass wrap the outside. Inside: a wine fridge, a hot-cold-sparkling water faucet, and a cedar ceiling with lit shelving. Walk around the other side and you're in a powder room with a backlit arched mirror.

The stone became the thread connecting old and new at Sunridge. It runs from the kitchen through the stairs and into the living space.

The logs were staying. That was the starting point. Sunridge is a timber log home in Whistler. Every design decision started with: how does this sit beside a round timber beam?

In the kitchen, white oak and black oak cabinetry meet the original log wall. That junction had to feel effortless. The marble island grounds the space. Natural stone, modern lighting, and clean lines complement the heritage structure rather than fight it.

Six Bathrooms, One Thread

8 slides · Narrative arc: black freestanding tub with breccia sarda → double waterfall vanity → guest bath with limestone and finger tile → walk-in shower with backlit niche → vanity detail with oak and black hardware → second shower with different tile → guest freestanding tub → the powder room, number six

Five bathrooms and a powder room. Each one different. All of them connected. The constants: natural limestone, black Fantini fixtures, backlit mirrors, black wall sconces. Everything else shifts room to room. Finger tile and oak in one. Chapel-quality porcelain in another. Breccia sarda and a freestanding black tub in the primary.

Enough consistency to feel like one house. Enough variation to make each room its own moment.

The primary suite at Sunridge is where we got to push the materials the furthest. Breccia sarda wraps the wet areas. A freestanding black tub sits near the window with alabaster sconces and towel warmers. The steam shower glows with under-bench and niche lighting. Next door, a custom cedar sauna with the same level of intention.

We expanded the bedroom windows to bring in more forest. The wardrobe is custom oak shelving under a vaulted window. Every odd corner in this suite got worked into something useful.

The Renovation Challenge
Sunridge is a timber log home in Whistler. The logs were staying. Every design decision started with: how does this sit beside a round timber beam?

In the kitchen, new oak meets the original log wall. That junction had to feel effortless. A round stone structure anchors the center of the house, running from the kitchen through the staircase and into the living space. It became the thread connecting old and new.

Renovation work is different from new builds. You're designing around something that already has a voice. Our job was to add to the conversation without talking over it.

Photography: Matt Anthony

Supporting Local Artists
Two lighting pieces at Sunridge are by local BC artists: Bachi for the living room chandelier, Matthew McCormick for the entry pendant. Both made original pieces for the home.

The Bachi piece catches light from the double-height windows and scatters it across the stone fireplace. Soft, organic, completely at home beside the log beams. The McCormick piece is more structural, but has the same quality of being made by someone who understands materials.

When you bring a maker's work into a home, it creates a connection to place you can't get from a catalog. The work is better for it.
