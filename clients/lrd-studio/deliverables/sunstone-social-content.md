## CAROUSEL POST 1 — The Kitchen Story (8 slides)
**Platform:** Instagram
**Pillar:** Project Showcase

### Caption:
Not everyone wants their everyday dishes behind a door.

These clients welcomed that. A long exposed shelf runs the length of the kitchen, holding the bowls and cups they actually reach for every morning. It's not about display. It's about making the rhythm of your day feel effortless.

Underneath, there's an in-set metal reveal between the countertop and the wood band on the lower cabinets. It's a detail most people won't notice, but it gives the whole composition a crispness that you feel even if you can't name it.

Around the corner, there's a hidden pantry. No door. You wouldn't know it's there. But step inside and you've got a full-height wine fridge and all the storage you'd ever need, completely concealed from the main space.

This is Sunstone, up high above the valley in the hills of Pemberton. Western Living Design 25 winner, 2026.

## CAROUSEL POST 2 — The Details That Disappear (8 slides)
**Platform:** Instagram
**Pillar:** Material & Detail

### Slide sequence + photo mapping:

| 1 | 7069 (staircase with figure) | Hero: motion on the stairs |

| 5 | 6976 (wardrobe underlit) | Wardrobe with underlit shelving |
| 6 | 6986 (office daybed) | Integrated daybed with storage |
| 7 | 7273 (mudroom) | Mudroom, everything in its place |
| 8 | 7299 (entry close-up) | Back to the entry. Full circle. |

### Caption:
The things that disappear are the things we think about the most.

The shelf at the entry looks simple. But underneath, there's a hidden key drawer with a mitered top. You open it from the bottom and you'd never know it was there.

The powder room tile looks like a single pattern. Look closer. It's a matte chevron with an irregular texture, so the surface shifts and breathes with the light.

In the primary bedroom, the nightstands aren't nightstands. They're custom C-shaped integrated pieces that float off the headboard. No legs. No cords. Just a surface exactly where you need it.

The wardrobe has a pull-out folding shelf. Instead of folding clothes on the bed and carrying them over, you fold them right where they're going.

Every detail in this house was designed to feel invisible. That's the whole point.
