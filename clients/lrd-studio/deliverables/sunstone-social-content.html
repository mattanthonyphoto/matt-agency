The things that disappear are the things we think about the most. A hidden key drawer under the entry shelf. Mitered top, opens from the bottom. You'd never know.

Matte chevron tile in the powder room with an irregular texture that shifts with the light. C-shaped nightstands that float off the headboard. A pull-out folding shelf in the wardrobe so you fold things right where they're going.

Award Announcement
We're honored to share that Sunstone has been recognized with the Western Living Design 25 Award for 2026.

This home in Pemberton, BC was a collaboration with Dorgelo by Design, photographed by Darby Magill. Our clients welcomed a dramatic flair, and together we created something we're genuinely proud of.

The kitchen's exposed shelf, the hidden pantry, the custom asymmetrical fireplace, the staircase with its wood caps and metal fins, the ensuite with mountains through the window. Every detail was designed to feel intentional without being precious.

Thank you to Western Living and to the clients who trusted us with their home.
Design Process · Educational
One of the details I love most at Sunstone is the entry shelf. It looks simple. A floating surface, a mirror, a place for flowers.

Underneath, there's a hidden key drawer. The top is mitered so the seam is invisible. You open it from the bottom. You'd never know it was there.

This is what I mean when I say design is equal parts logic and creative. The creative part is the composition. The logic part is asking: where do the keys actually go? Where does the phone charge? What happens when you walk in the door at 9pm with groceries and boots?

The best details are the ones that disappear.
