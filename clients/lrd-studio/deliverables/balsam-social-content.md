## CAROUSEL POST 1 — The Kitchen Story (8 slides)
**Platform:** Instagram
**Pillar:** Project Showcase
**Voice:** Lauren (first person plural)

### Slide sequence + photo mapping:

| Slide | Photo | What it shows |
|---|---|---|
| 1 | 0441 (kitchen wide) | Hero: the full kitchen with cold rolled steel hood and sloped ceiling |
| 2 | 0531 (kitchen island straight-on) | The island, four stools, glass pendants, professional range |
| 3 | 0541 (island end detail) | The rustic oak texture and glass-front wine cabinet |
| 4 | 0491 (island built-in wine fridge) | Built-in glassware display at the island end |
| 5 | 0627 (island bar detail) | The oak panels up close, bread on the cutting board |
| 6 | 0639 (kitchen alternate angle) | Glass shaker cabinets flanking the steel hood |
| 7 | 0450 (dining room) | The dining room just beyond, live edge walnut table |
| 8 | 0555 (wine room) | The glass wine room, a few steps away |

### Caption:
This kitchen has a very tall sloped ceiling at the top of it, and that height needed an anchor. So we layered cold rolled steel with horizontal framework for the hood fan, and carried that same steel into the backsplash behind the stove.

Flanking that area, we used chisel limestone. All of those features are stacked on top of a ledge to the countertop, so there's a really easy surface to clean for years to come. But you've got this wonderful blend of textures with the metal and the stone.

Then there's a mix of glass shaker cabinets to feature the glassware, and flat panel textured oak. A brushed, more rugged, rustic oak that gives the whole kitchen its warmth. The pantry to the side has this French door with black framework. And the broom closet around the corner from the fridge is totally concealed by flat panel millwork. You wouldn't know it's there.

This is Balsam, in Whistler. One of our favorite kitchens to date.

### Caption:
Next to the kitchen there's this jewel box of a wine room. It's about five by five, basically a display case for the collection. We repeated the chisel limestone behind the back wall, mounted the bottles horizontal on vertical brass hardware brackets, and lined it with cove lighting. It glows.

The L-shaped bar inside is a layered butcher block cascading over the countertop material. When you open the full expansion sliding doors to the deck just outside the kitchen, the whole space becomes one big entertaining room.

And then downstairs, there's this bar in the media room that opens up to an accordion window with a bar top. The clients affectionately call it the teaky lounge, so we got them a custom light that says exactly that.

This home was designed around gathering. Every room leads to the next, and every threshold invites you through.

**Platform:** Instagram

| 7 | 0683 (staircase looking up) | Looking up through glass to wine room beyond |
| 8 | 0723 (powder room) | The powder room as a hidden moment |

### Caption:
The things we spend the most time on are the things you're not supposed to notice.

The stairs are really lovely. Quite simple. But each step is underlit, so when you move through the house at night, there's this warm glow guiding you up. They have a seamless simplicity to them that we worked hard to achieve.

The entry had an interesting angled adjacency to the stairs and the garage, so we decided to follow the floor line with that angle. All the tiles are angled in the entry, which is a detail that feels inevitable once you see it. The cabinetry is inset with adjacent wall paneling, and you wouldn't know it was there if there weren't handles on it.

The wardrobe is all open concept, with the rods underlit. It sounds like a small thing, but when you're getting ready in the morning, it changes the experience entirely.

We believe the best design decisions are the ones that disappear into how a home actually works.

## CAROUSEL POST 5 — The Powder Room & Stone Story (6 slides)
**Platform:** Instagram
**Pillar:** Material & Detail
**Voice:** Lauren

### Slide sequence + photo mapping:

| Slide | Photo | What it shows |
|---|---|---|
| 1 | 0723 (powder room) | Hero: ocean black natural stone, backlit mirror, carmana vanity |
| 2 | 0544 (fireplace close) | Kingston slate fireplace detail, oak mantel, firewood storage |
| 3 | 0552 (living room wide) | Full living room with Kingston slate as anchor |
| 4 | 0569 (ensuite tub) | Breccia sarda accent wall behind the black tub |
| 5 | 0441 (kitchen) | Chisel limestone flanking the steel hood |
| 6 | 0555 (wine room) | Chisel limestone repeated in the wine room |

### Caption:
Every stone in this home tells a different story.

The powder room wall is ocean black natural stone. It has this depth and movement that shifts depending on where you're standing. Paired with the backlit mirror and the floating carmana vanity, it became one of my favorite rooms in the entire house.

The fireplace is Kingston slate, which is from Ontario where the clients were originally from. We loved that it connected them to home in a way that's quiet and personal. Nobody would know unless you told them.

In the kitchen, chisel limestone flanks the cold rolled steel hood. We repeated it in the wine room behind the bottle racks. That repetition ties the two spaces together without making them feel the same.

Working with natural stone means every slab is different. You can't predict exactly what it will look like installed. That's the whole point. The material tells you what it wants to be.
