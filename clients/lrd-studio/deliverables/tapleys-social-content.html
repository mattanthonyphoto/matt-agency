The Fireplace with Wood Fins The living room fireplace at Tapleys has this design detail we stumbled on and then couldn't let go of. Wood fins on either side, framing the hearth.

It was one of those discoveries that happens mid-process, when you're working through the build and something unexpected clicks. We love that about renovation work. The existing structure pushes you somewhere you wouldn't have gone in a new build, and it's often better for it.
Photography @mattanthonyphoto · Build @tymacconstruction
#fireplacedesign #interiordesign #whistler #wooddetail #customfireplace #seatosky #homerenovation
The Modernized Staircase These stairs were existing. We modernized them with a boxy top cap, glass panels, and then took the original handrail, had it refinished, and added a light underneath.

That's it. No structural changes. But the transformation was complete. Sometimes the simplest moves carry the most weight, especially in a renovation where honoring the bones of a home is the whole point.
Photography @mattanthonyphoto · Build @tymacconstruction
#staircasedesign #homerenovation #interiordesign #whistler #seatosky #modernstairs #renovationtransformation
The Hidden Coffee Nook There's this coffee nook tucked around the corner from the kitchen. It's hidden from the main space but still exposed, not behind a door. One of those in-between spots that becomes the favorite spot.

We find that the moments people love most in a home aren't the grand ones. They're the 7am ones, the quiet corners where the morning routine starts before anyone else is up.

Honoring Existing Materials
We just wrapped the photography for Tapleys, a major renovation in Whistler we designed with Tymac Construction and photographed by Matt Anthony.

The palette started with a question: what do we do with the fir? The tone had aged into a deep orange the family had outgrown. We had it all refinished with a whitewash — lighter, but the grain and character are still there. From there, hickory floors, black Carmana stone from Vancouver Island, organic zellige, black and brass hardware by M-Tack.

Renovation design works within constraints, and those constraints push you somewhere more interesting. The best renovation work doesn't erase a home's history. It brings it forward.

Photography by Matt Anthony. Build by Tymac Construction.

Designing for Multigenerational Living
We're seeing more families design for the long view.

At Tapleys in Whistler, the clients asked for a parent suite with its own kitchenette, ensuite, and wardrobe. Not a guest room. A fully self-contained living space designed so that family could live there comfortably for years.

The rest of the house reflects that same generosity. The boys chose the teal tones throughout. A game closet hides behind a slatted panel. Every space designed for real life.

Multigenerational design isn't a trend. It's a return to how homes were always meant to work.
