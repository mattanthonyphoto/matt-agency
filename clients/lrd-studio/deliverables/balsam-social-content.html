The Material Story
We just wrapped Balsam, a new build in Whistler designed from the ground up with TYMAC Contracting.

Cold rolled steel for the kitchen hood. Chisel limestone flanking the range and repeated in the wine room. Kingston slate from Ontario for the fireplace, connecting the clients to home. Every room has a moment where two or three materials meet.

The best spaces aren't about any single finish. They're about how honest materials interact.

Photography by Darby Magill · Build by TYMAC Contracting · Custom furniture by Lock & Mortise and Benson · lrdstudio.ca/projects/residential/balsam
The Concealed Design · Educational
One of the things we think about a lot at LRD Studio is what to hide.

At Balsam in Whistler, the broom closet is concealed behind flat panel millwork. Entry cabinetry reads as architecture, not storage. A closet door in the media room disappears into the wall. The wardrobe rods are underlit.

The most thoughtful design decisions are the ones nobody notices. When everything functional feels invisible, what's left is a home that simply works.
